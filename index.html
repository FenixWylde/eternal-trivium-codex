Teaser In a world where magic is memory spent, three orphaned siblings are the reincarnated gods who once died to save it — and they don’t remember why.
Back Cover On the world of Vi’talitas, magic is Animancy — the art of burning your own memories for power, paid not in mana but in the feeling you lose forever. When a tear in reality begins flooding the world with Stigma, a corruption that hollows souls into monsters, the only ones who can seal it are three siblings raised in a home for lost children: Omin, who traded her sight to perceive the unseen; Amoz, who devours the wicked to free their souls; and I’onnie, who hears the dead but is deaf to the living. They are the last incarnations of the fallen god-souls who lost the first war — and to win this one, they must Recall the lives they died living. An epic of memory, mercy, and the cost of power.
The Deeper Hook The Great Mother A’kana gave her children the gift of Anima — the power to know and tend a soul — and was betrayed twice: once by her brother A’thule, who forged Stigma to unmake creation, and once from within, when one of her own protectors corrupted the divine Eversouls in a war that broke the world. A’kana spent the last of herself sealing the wound, scattering her surviving children into mortal rebirth with their memories wiped. Generations later, the seal is failing. The three who must mend it — the Ra’quiss siblings — live as ordinary, distrusted Medians, casters who pull power from their own past lives at the price of greying away, memory by memory, toward the same Stigma they’re sworn to fight. As they uncover what they were, they inherit not just power but guilt: the betrayer was family, the war was kin against kin, and the mercy they deliver to the corrupted — releasing trapped souls back to the cycle — is the same mercy one sister once gave the brother she was forced to strike down. Eternal Trivium braids a coming-of-age, a slow-burn tragedy of reincarnated love, and a cosmology where the only cure for corruption is to return to where you began.
Title & Ciphering Locked Series / world title: Eternal Trivium (no “The”). Names the whole structure of reality — the three-Pillar cosmos governed by the Twelve Laws — so every story sits beneath it. Iconic shape (adjective + weighty noun), a canon term rather than a bolted-on name, clean and sayable, and it quietly encodes the “three” that organizes the world. Animancy is the magic within the world (its “Force”), not the title. Three-tier brand: world title → magic system → book subtitles (e.g. Eternal Trivium: Sea of Lights).

Genre & Themes Locked

Eternal Trivium is Mythic Afrofantasy — a character-driven secondary world with its own metaphysics, gods, and afterlife, drawn from an African-diasporic mythic imagination. That imagination is rooted in the author’s own heritage: the traditions of the American South and of Jamaica — two branches of the same diaspora, from Hoodoo, Gullah, and spiritual folkways to Anansi, duppy-lore, and Caribbean cosmology — woven together as the wellspring of Vi’talitas. It is a Reincarnation Saga at its spine, carried by an Aetherpunk register where soul-energy powers both relic and machine, set against a Parallel World reborn from the heart of a fallen god. Its emotional key is one of Metaphysical Wonder — braiding a coming-of-age, a slow-burn romantic tragedy of reincarnated love, and the cosmic-horror edge of Stigma and the Veils. At its heart it is a story about memory, grief, mercy, and the cost of power.

Voice & representation LockedThe prose centers Black, PoC, and LGBTQ+ characters and perspectives as a core, non-incidental part of the world — their cultures, relationships, and interiority written with depth and dignity rather than as token presence. The book is Black-, PoC-, and queer-friendly by design, both in who its heroes are and in how their stories are told.

A singular, boundless consciousness — “Fate” or “Destiny” in mortal terms. All and nothing, everywhere and nowhere, beyond beginning or ending. To stand before it is to stand at the edge of all Truths. It understood that true life and true death require true balance — and so divided itself into structure.

The Twelve Eternal Laws Locked

If the Trivium is the structure, the Twelve Laws are its constitution — the principles that maintain balance across the three dimensions (Mind, Body, Soul), four to each. They are taught as maxims a Median lives and trains by — practical wisdom and cosmic principle at once.

Who keeps them LockedThe Eversouls upheld the Twelve collectively — not one keeper per Law, but a shared upkeep, like a choir holding a chord. When the Body line fell and the Spirit line broke, no single Law snapped; the whole balance thinned for want of keepers. This is why the present world slips even though the Laws still stand.
Laws of the Mind
  • The Memory is the Source — Memory is what Anima draws from; the root of all casting.
  • Prepare the Mind, the Body will follow — mental discipline precedes physical power.
  • Emotion does not equate to fact — feeling is not truth; the Neurolist’s discipline and a rebuke of Stigma.
  • Sight is not truth — perception deceives. The Law Silas (Illusion) violates; echoed in the prophecy’s “Believe not what your plain eyes see.”
Laws of the Body
  • Action is always louder than thought — the Body’s counterweight to the Mind; the vessel speaks.
  • The vessel does not forget — memory lives in flesh as well as mind (pairs with “The Memory is the Source”).
  • Revere your flesh. Let it speak for you — the body as honest expression.
  • What is broken still serves — the flawed vessel is the truest; the doomed-born carry the strongest will. (The protagonists embodied.)
Laws of the Soul — in A’kana’s own words
  • The Living will Die — no incarnate state is permanent.
  • The Dead will Live — death is not an end; the dead return to living.
  • From Anima we Emerge; To Anima we Return — the conservation Law that Stigma violates by refusing return.
  • So thy Soul will always Endure — the soul persists. Comfort to the faithful, sentence to the damned (Silas endures; A’thule endures).

How the Laws function LockedOrdinary Animancy operates within the Laws. Bending or breaking a Law is the source of forbidden power and a doorway for Stigma. The corrupted don’t break assigned Laws — they fail the balance their dimension was meant to keep (the fallen Body line: Desire, Ego, Morality, Integrity unbalanced; Silas violating Sight is not truth).

The Immortal Fissure Locked renamed: was “Eternal Cataclysm”

A’thule, ruler of the Mind, came to covet his siblings’ contentment and the Omneity’s authority. He struck first at A’kana, believing her benevolence made her weak. A’thesis intervened and was destroyed defending her; his remains were cast adrift among the Pillars. Enraged, A’kana spent vast energy to tear A’thule from the Astroverse and seal him in a parallel plane just outside the three Pillars — the False Pillars — fortified with Aether.

A’kana’s Slumber, the Polestar & Ne’haris Locked

Weakened beyond repair, A’kana created a sanctuary deep in the Pneumaverse. Her incorporeal body became The Polestar — a core of raw, chaotic, radiant aether — which birthed the realm Ne’haris, haven to the Unborn, Unliving, and Unseen, the divine nucleus of the Pneumaverse. There she sleeps, beyond the reach of A’thule’s taint.

The Aether Crux — The Great Mother Tree Locked

A’kana’s sleeping essence is also a living World Tree. The Aether Crux (Anima Crux, Tree of Life) is the sentient remaining essence of A’kana of Many Souls — the source of Ne’haris and a system of webbed tendrils spanning the world. Mortals know her as Yggdrasil, the Kabbalah, the World Tree; to Ne’haris she is simply “Great Mother A’kana.” Beyond white — luminance, radiating living light; her bark and leaves make the finest healing remedies known.

  • Beacon of Anima: saturated with soul-energy, she flows Anima out through her roots into worlds beyond — lending influence to all worlds she connects to.
  • The Leylines are her roots: the Ley Lines are the Roots of A’kana, like blood vessels. A new world gets a root that feeds it Anima; the network connects all living worlds to the Soul Space, recycling energy for Balance. Sever a root or kill a world, and it withers back to the Soul Realm.
  • The Aurora: the Crux birthed the Northern Lights — a gateway into the Sea of Lights through which souls traverse.
  • Nine Rune-Carved Obelisks: rune-carved wards around the tree, one for each of the Nine Ciphers of Anima, representing the pathways of Matter, Anti-Matter, and Dark Matter.
Why A’thule is limited LockedA’kana is mother of all Anima — every pathway is hers to govern, even the Anima A’thule wields. This is why A’thule can use only a fraction of his full power: her hold over the Anima Domains throttles him. Even sleeping, the Great Mother keeps her hand on the betrayer’s strength.

The Eversouls — A’kana’s Children Locked

To defend the Pneumaverse, A’kana created the Eversouls — her children, instrumental to the founding of Ne’haris and protectors of its deepest secrets. They are a distinct tier of being: below the Paramounts but far above ordinary souls.

Eighteen, then seventeen LockedA’kana tailored eighteen Eversouls — but A’sai was never born (slain in incubation by Silas), leaving seventeen active. The Eversouls are not the Ciphers themselves; they are the smaller constituent parts of the nine Ciphers of Anima — finer Aspects of the Soul that, together, compose each Cipher. So the nine Ciphers and the eighteen Eversouls are two scales of the same system: the Cipher is the whole, the Eversouls its facets.

The Nature of the Eversouls Locked · New

Beyond their roster and their fall, the Eversouls are a living order of being — ancient, elemental, and deeply bonded. What follows is their ecology as the oldest lore records it.

Lifespan & rebirth LockedEversouls live immense lifespans — on the order of 1,000 to 12,000 Aeons, though even that may understate it, given how much of their existence is spent within the Sea of Lights. In time an Eversoul’s Essence can lose its vibrancy; they then wither and fall — but, like their Fonixi descendants, they can be reborn. A reborn Eversoul may choose to take a mortal form or return to their Essence within the Sea of Lights.
Essence & element LockedThough not strictly Elemental, each Eversoul holds sway over the element bound to their Essence (their Anima Cipher) — the finer Aspect they embody expressed as command over its matching force. Their Essence is the very power they carry: a compound of spiritual and elemental force, tied to their psionic nature, and wholly immaterial — it makes up the entirety of their being.
How they multiply — and the first Phoenixes LockedEversouls do not breed. They regard one another as kin — brothers and sisters — and instead reproduce by parting with a sliver of their own Essence to create another of their kind. This progeny does not carry the full strength of its sire; such lesser-born are more material than their parent and more common within the Sea of Lights. It is said this is how the first Phoenixes (the Fonixi line) came into existence.
Description LockedAn Eversoul takes a vast, radiant shape — often read as an enormous phoenix — but rather than flame they are shrouded in spiritual, elemental energy. From afar they seem to have no body at all: vibrant lights dancing in the distance, feathers glittering in flight, their color matching the Essence they embody. Their forms are near-immaterial, able to slip through the narrowest passages. Those who meet one up close find talons like diamond and eyes and beak hued to the rest of their light. An Eversoul will not strike first unprovoked — but if challenged, defends with devastating psionic, spiritual, and elemental force.
Personality LockedAs beings of psionic, spiritual, and elemental power, Eversouls are attuned to the mind, soul, and raw energy of those they meet. Like the Empyrons they are creatures of powerful emotion — but where the Empyrons alter their surroundings, Eversouls can alter those near them, their moods becoming the moods of nearby living things. Believing deeply in balance, this sway runs either way, positive or negative — making them remarkable empaths. Generally neutral, neither good nor evil, reclusive and often travelling; one is far likelier to meet a Phoenix than an Eversoul. They do not seek conflict but become adversaries of terrible force when provoked. Wary of beings that lean to the extremes of their nature, they are nonetheless fascinated by mortal races — known to quietly orbit the mortal world, observing through psionic sense and speaking to the sensitive through telepathy.
Society LockedThe Eversouls see themselves as brothers and sisters, divided by the three lines. They travel in pairs or alone; only in the rarest moments do they gather as a full company — and then they are at their most powerful. They may disagree, but their bond is near-unbreakable: if one is harmed, the others feel the pain; if one falls, all mourn; when one returns, all rejoice. Gathered together, they were known to sing soulful melodies that filled the Sea of Lights with harmony. Since their fall, no such song has been heard.
Relations & homelands LockedBeings held to descend from the Eversouls include the Fonixi (Phoenixes), Thunderbirds, Rocs, Firebirds, and lesser avians. Their origin is legend: some say they emerged from the prismatic mists of the Sea of Lights and simply took shape; either way, Ne’haris was their true home and gathering-place. Being nomadic, they kept no single residence — but there was one hidden place they returned to, to share knowledge, birth their progeny, and retire to when it was time to wither and fall: Prama, the Reliquary of Truth and Knowledge, a sanctuary reachable only by other winged beings or the Eversouls themselves.
Material — shed Essence & the Eversoul Core LockedAn Eversoul’s Essence sheds naturally, like a feather, once it has lingered in a place; where it settles it crystallizes into Hymnstones — translucent stones lit with the hue of the Essence they fell from, carrying both elemental and spiritual properties. They are rare, coveted, and dangerous to seek, feeding a black market that partly explains Eversoul elusiveness. Rarer still is the Eversoul Core: when an Eversoul falls, it leaves behind a smooth sphere, as large as a dragon’s egg, bursting with its remaining Essence. Its power is temporary — harvesters have only so long before it returns to the mists — and only a skilled Enchanter can imbue its energy into a lasting item. Botched, the Core’s energies turn on the user’s mind or soul in ways shaped by the Essence it came from.

The Betrayal of Silas Locked

In the War for Ne’haris, many Eversouls were Stigmatized — through an inside betrayal by one of their own brothers, Silas. This is the cosmology’s second betrayal-by-kin, rhyming one tier down from A’thule: betrayal-by-family is the recurring engine from gods to children to mortals. It also resolves how Ne’haris was wounded — from within, by one of its protectors. With particular cruelty, Silas killed his sister A’sai in incubation, before she could be born (her status, Unborn, is literal) — yet she arrived anyway as I’onnie.

The Unresolved War Locked as ambiguous

A’thule’s forces warred to seize Ne’haris — and through it, A’kana’s essence and endless aether. How the war ended is deliberately unknown in-world: some say Ne’haris fell, some say it was saved, some say A’kana abandoned it. This ambiguity is a feature, not a gap.

Vi’talitas — the Bridge Locked

The world of Vi’talitas is reborn from the core of a Paramount — A’kana’s renewal. This is why the story happens here: the planet is the renewing heart of the Great Mother, the very prize A’thule seeks. Three siblings — the prophesied Ra’quisss (see §6) — orchestrate its renewal toward the future of the Trivium.

Resolved LockedThe three are the Ra’quiss siblings of the Whispers of the Mother, each embodying one of Ne’haris’s trinity (Unborn / Unliving / Unseen) — not mirrors of the Paramounts.

Anima & Animancy

Anima
The engine, the Nine Ciphers, and soul access

Anima — the Source Locked

Anima is inherent and tied to the intensity of the soul. One soul lives many lives; each adds experience. The more lives lived, the more powerful the soul and the greater its base anima pool. Young souls have little; some have none.

Origin of Anima LockedAnima is the “magic” substance of the world — a natural element permeating the entire Pneumaverse, the stuff the Aegis Grid is made of and that A’kana’s aura radiates. It originated from A’kana as a gift: the means to navigate the existence and makeup of a Soul. So Animancy is not stolen or transgressive — it is a birthright of the Pneumaverse, the Great Mother’s endowment. (A sacred gift, not a theft.)
The Eye of the Aegis LockedAnima is produced at the Eye of the Aegis — the center and core of Vi’talitas, where the Great Mother Tree stands. From this core, Anima wells up and pushes outward through A’kana’s roots (the Leylines) into the Aegis Grid and the worlds beyond. Because Vi’talitas is Ne’haris, the Eye is the single point where the Tree, the world’s core, and the wellspring of all Anima coincide — the beating heart of the cosmos’s soul-economy.

SoulCraft — the Eversouls’ Art Locked

The old name for Animancy LockedSoulCraft is the ancient name for Animancy. Before the present age, the Eversouls did not call their art “Animancy” — they called it SoulCraft. The two are the same discipline across two ages: what the Eversouls practiced as SoulCraft is what Medians now practice, in diminished form, as Animancy.

When A’kana made the Eversouls, she gifted each a power that amplifies the Aspect they embody — a SoulCraft: a personal style of anima-based attack and expression. To receive it, an Eversoul undergoes a ritual that bonds and infuses the craft into an object — their Animatrux, a living crystallized energy source that serves as the key power-source of that Eversoul, keeping their aura perpetually at arms.

  • SoulCrafts take elemental and conceptual forms — Shadowcraft, Flamecraft, Terracraft, Lightcraft, and many more.
  • Though mastered most fully by the Eversouls, SoulCraft is not theirs alone — beings across the Fields can learn it. One must first know which Pillar their Anima falls under, then train to hone the Anima a given craft requires.
Energy is a fluxEnergy flows; it cannot die — only transmute and return, trapped and freed at once. All energy issues from the Soul: souls create energy, and energy is soul. To draw out this ancient, primal energy at will is to bend Aether — soul-energy itself.

Auric Remanence — the Eighth Sense Locked

Every race on Vi’talitas possesses Auric Remanence as a literal sense — alongside sight, sound, scent, taste, touch, movement, body-limit, and message-interpretation. It is the soul’s retained reservoir of past-life experience — the residue that remains after each life’s field has passed — and the ability to identify memories from past lives and call upon them for present use. It is the sensory faculty that underlies all memory-casting: Animancy is Auric Remanence, trained and spent.

How a soul finds its Cipher LockedThrough Auric Remanence, the soul retains the emotional experiences that left the most defined impressions upon it across its many lifetimes. Some emotions are more deeply ingrained in a given soul than others — and through that dominant emotional register, the soul becomes associated with a particular Cipher of Anima. The Feii are its master-teachers, their connection to the Nyph tying the elemental peoples to this lineage.

The Nkra — the Soul’s Nucleus Locked · New

Every soul carries a Nkra — the nucleus of what it is. The Nkra holds all that soul has ever experienced across every life it has lived, and it is what allows rebirth to move forward at all. Because no two souls have lived the same lives, no two Nkra are alike: it is at once a soul’s memory, its inheritance, and its signature. The message and the fingerprint are not two things — the signature is what the message looks like from outside.

A’kana’s gift Locked · NewThe Nkra is universal — every soul has one, without exception. The Eversouls held it sacred: each soul’s gift to existence from A’kana herself, the Great Mother’s portion in every living thing. Nkra is the ancient word for the message a soul carries out of death and into the next life.
Nkra produces Anima — and makes Medians Locked · NewNkra is what produces Anima. This is the root of the Median/Generic divide: those with potent Nkra become Medians, and even among them potency varies widely — some far more powerful than others. The Ra’quiss siblings sit at the extreme of that range. It also restates the locked rule that Anima follows the intensity of the soul: more lives lived means a denser Nkra, and a denser Nkra means more Anima to draw on.
Reading, shielding, and flaunting
Nkra is perceptible — which makes its handling a matter of manners as much as skill.
Read by trained Service
Perceiving another’s Nkra is a trainable discipline, not a rare gift — the province of Soothsayers and Veilwalkers especially. Omin reads it as she reads all things: through Aether remnants.
Shielded by choice
A soul can hide its Nkra to avoid being read. This is ordinary and unremarkable — a potent Nkra can be genuinely overwhelming to those sensitive to it, and shielding is simple consideration.
Flaunting is belligerence
To leave a potent Nkra open and unshielded in company is considered aggressive — a deliberate imposition on everyone present. Among Medians it reads as a threat, a challenge, or gross ill-breeding.
The Reset — Stigma’s true cost Locked · NewStigma does not merely madden the mind; it corrupts the Nkra itself. Carried to completion, the Nkra is reset — wiped to a blank nucleus, losing everything the soul ever experienced and beginning again as a wholly new soul. This is the true horror beneath Stigmatization: not death, which every soul survives, but the ending of the thing that carried you through death.
Cleansing prevents the Reset Locked · NewThe Reset is a process, not an instant — and it can be interrupted. A soul cleansed of Stigma before the Reset completes keeps its Nkra intact, losing nothing of what it has lived. This is the general rule, and it is why cleansing is urgent rather than merely merciful: every hour a Stigmatized soul goes uncleansed is continuity burning away. Once the Reset finishes, there is nothing left to cleanse — only a blank soul wearing no history at all.
Why Maisel is extraordinary Locked · NewThe reset is a process, not an instant — which is the whole weight of Mulu’lizi’s rescue. Fully Stigmatized at death, she was cleansed by D’vita before the reset completed, and so her Nkra survived intact. This is precisely why she is the only sibling D’vita could save, why her labor in the Sea took centuries, and why — as Maisel — her old life fractures and resurfaces at all. Nothing was erased. It was very nearly lost.
Greying, reconsidered Locked · NewBecause casting spends Recalled memory and memory lives in the Nkra, Animancy draws directly on the nucleus. A greyed memory — colour that never fully returns — is Nkra erosion: not merely a feeling a Median can no longer reach, but a fragment burned out of the thing that carries them forward. A veteran’s dulled Signets map, quite literally, what they have spent of their own continuity.
What Silas is actually hunting Locked · NewSilas does not track people across lifetimes; he tracks Nkra. The prophecy fragments the siblings carry are held within their Nkra by nature and Path — which is why assembling the three is the thing he must prevent. His three failures restate cleanly: Omin and Amoz are illegible, their Nkra scrambled by the improbable Ra’gnn/Fonixi cross; Ion is invisible, a Nkra with no prior life accumulated in it and therefore nothing to read.
Design payoffNkra unifies four systems that were previously described separately — Anima’s source, the Median/Generic divide, greying, and Silas’s soul-scanning — under a single named object, without altering any of their locked mechanics. It also raises the stakes of Stigma from madness to annihilation of continuity, and hands the world a ready-made social register: shielding as courtesy, flaunting as aggression.

Casting — Recall & Release Locked

A Median casts by Recalling a memory (surfacing it through Auric Remanence until its charge is raw) and Releasing that charge through a Cipher, which shapes it into an effect — simply Recall → Release. Memory intensity equals cast intensity. The memory is the fuel; the Cipher is the lens (any memory can fuel any Cipher). The cost is felt, not numeric: Releasing a memory means temporarily losing access to its emotional charge. The act is fundamentally internal — the Median reaches inward rather than relying on an external mechanism.

The visible signature — the Signets LockedCasting is subtractive, and the signature is the cost made visible. Where other powers add light to the world, Animancy briefly takes light from the caster. Every Median bears Signets — Anima-marks placed on the body at awakening, their location fixed by Cipher so placement announces a Median’s discipline (Origin on the face, Qhaos on the back, etc.; see the Cipher Resonance table). Aether is the exception, surfacing where the Median channels best. They lie dormant until casting.
The Cast Cycle
Recall → Release → Recovery — one visible cascade of color leaving and returning.
Recall
Color drains from the body and refocuses into the Signets and eyes, which ignite in the Cipher’s color. The eyes take the Cipher hue regardless of natural color: sclera and iris both flood with it, leaving only the pupil as a point of normal black or white (by race). The Median’s hair also shifts to the Cipher’s color for the cast. No white remains in the eye — a striking, faintly inhuman look.
Release
The gathered charge fires through the Cipher into the effect.
Recovery
Color bleeds back into the body; Signets and eyes cool to normal. Recovery speed = power — brief for a master, lingering for the weak or overdrawn.
Two tells, read at different moments LockedLoud-novice / quiet-master reads during Release: a novice leaks, the Signet-mark blazing wild; a master bleeds almost nothing, the mark a near-silent whisper. Drain-and-recovery reads after: a master is quick in the return; the weak stay greyed. The axes aren’t redundant — one reads in the firing, one in the aftermath. A true master is quiet in both.
Greying made visible LockedBecause casting is “color leaves, then returns,” a greyed memory is one whose color never fully comes back. Overspend, and a Signet stays dull, an eye stays washed-out — a permanent, readable scar. A veteran’s Signets map their history of loss; a sharp onlooker can read how much a Median has spent of themselves off their own body.
Omin — the signature in full Locked
The complete drain-and-Signet cascade is precisely her tell.

The Aetherseer casts with the full cycle — drain, Signets and eyes alight in Aether’s gold, recovery. The poignancy of how a sightless caster’s lit eyes read — what it means to blaze with a light she cannot see — is reserved for her character pass.

The Sequence — The Recall State Locked

Casting is not a single instantaneous act but an entered state that can then be spent from repeatedly — like a fighter’s powered-up form.

Ignite → Sustain → Release (repeatable)
Recall is ignition
Entering the charged state can be near-instant for the skilled — a master drops into Recall in a heartbeat; a novice is slow. Speed of entry is itself a measure of skill.
A sustained form
Once in the Recall State, the Median holds it — it persists for as long as their soul-stamina allows.
Release doesn’t end it
Firing a Release does not drop them out of Recall. They stay in the form and can Release as often as required while it holds — a barrage, not a single shot.
Savvy is the fuel
What limits the form is Savvy (soul-stamina). Each Release and moment held draws on it; when it runs low the form dulls and collapses. (See Vitalitas → Savvy, Game System.)
Endurance is trained
Holding the form longer and recovering Savvy faster is a discipline every Median must build — even masters worked to extend it. Soul age helps; endurance is earned.
Why it matters LockedThis turns casting from a per-shot transaction into a resource-managed state — a Median chooses when to enter the form, then fights from inside it until their Savvy gives out. Combat gains a rhythm: ignite → barrage → burn down → recover. Soul-stamina, not per-cast cost, is the real ceiling on power in a fight.

The Invocation — Naming the Working Locked · New

Recall is cognitive. What ignites the state is the act of naming the working in the mind — and a Median may leave it there, or put it in the mouth. Speaking is not required for the magic to function. It is required for the magic to be precise.

Names are taught, not confessed Locked · NewA working’s name is Institute vocabulary — formal, curricular, and shared. “Loving Embrace” is a Hallow spatial ward raising a shield over every ally within roughly ten feet; it is the name of the working, never of the caster’s private memory. Two Medians who have never met can both call Loving Embrace, fuel it from wholly different memories, and raise the same shield. The name points at the effect, not at the wound. This is why the Institutes can teach Animancy at all: the vocabulary is portable even though the fuel never is.
Two registers of the same name
Formal citation and field form — not two schools, but two levels of diction.
Formal — Auric Remanence: Loving Embrace
The full citation as it appears in an Institute manual. Used in training, examination, ceremony, and record — anywhere precision of reference matters more than speed.
Field — Loving Embrace
What is actually said in a fight. Auric Remanence is what every Median is doing; announcing it is like a soldier announcing “firearm” before firing. Dropping the prefix is fluency, not sloppiness — a Median who always cites in full reads as green, or as someone performing authority.
Silent — thought only
The name held in the mind and never spoken. Fully viable, and the ordinary practice of masters. Costs nothing but the margin described below.
What speech buys Locked · NewSpeaking the name aloud adds precision and reliability. It is the most dependable way to fix a working under pressure — the mind wanders, the mouth does not. A silent cast is not weaker in force, but it carries a wider margin of error: a slightly looser shape, a slightly less certain edge. Masters cast silent because they can afford that margin. Novices are taught to speak because they cannot.
Calling as escalation — the battlefield convention Locked · NewNaming aloud costs a beat. For a small working the beat is not worth it; for a devastating one — deep Recall, heavy Savvy, consequences a caster cannot afford to bungle — the beat buys certainty. The result is a convention every trained Median reads instantly: when a veteran speaks a name, the situation has changed. Silence is routine work. Speech means something large is coming, and allies should move accordingly.
Omin — the one part of her own casting she can perceive Locked · New
Silent alone; spoken with her team.

Omin sees only in Aether remnants — the auras of living and unliving things. She therefore cannot see her own drain: not the colour leaving her, not the Signets waking, not her eyes flooding Aether’s gold. The tell that every sighted Median reads on themselves is the one thing she is locked out of. The spoken name is the only part of her own casting she perceives.

Working alone, she casts silent — accepting the wider margin because no one needs to hear her. Leading her Leywarden team, she speaks, so they know what is coming and where to stand. Her voice is not a crutch; it is command.

Design payoffThe invocation layer extends the locked loud-novice / quiet-master axis outward by one ring: the novice leaks light and leaks words; the master is quiet in both. It also gives field scenes a free grammar of tension — a reader learns in two scenes that a spoken name means escalation, and feels it for the rest of the series without a line of explanation. And because names are Institute-taught rather than personal, an unnamed working immediately reads as improvised, private, or something nobody taught.

The Memory — Emotion Shapes the Cast Locked

A memory is not fuel of one flat kind. Every memory carries an emotion or sensation, and that feeling shapes the cast. Intensity of memory still sets intensity of effect — but the character of the feeling steers how that power expresses.

  • Tendencies, not a table. A memory invoking anger tends to increase intensity (force); one invoking calm tends to increase precision (control). Other feelings carry their own leanings — but two angry memories never produce identical spells. (Real rules, human cost, never arithmetic.)
  • Casting is autobiographical. The feeling is the steering, so a Median’s magic reflects who they have been. A caster who fights well on rage is a different person from one who fights on grief or joy.
  • Vs. Vitalitas: attributes are the baseline; emotion is the in-the-moment modulation. Tenacity sets how hard you can hit; casting on anger is how you lean into it now. They stack.
The core tension LockedThe most emotionally intense memories are the most powerful fuel and the most dangerous (see Mania). The best Medians aren’t those with the strongest memories — they’re those who can ride strong memories without drowning.

The Mania — Losing Oneself to the Memory Locked

Because casting means feeling, a Median can Recall too much — pulling a memory so deep it stops being a tool and becomes a place. They tip into Mania (distinct from the Berserker Role). It has two faces, and the memory’s emotion decides which strikes:

The two faces of Mania
Outward — loss of control
Memories that throw the cast outward (rage → force) throw the Median outward: the Cipher fires wild and uncommanded, no aim, no off-switch — a danger to allies and self. The body acts; the steering is gone.
Inward — loss of self
Memories that turn the cast inward (grief, calm) pull the Median inward: consciousness submerges into the memory, leaving a coma-like state — present in body, absent in mind, trapped reliving it. They must find their way out.
The two bulwarks LockedTwo Vitalitas attributes guard the two faces. Savvy holds the form (run out, the Recall State collapses); Actuality — perceive and assert what is real — anchors the self (run low, the memory swallows you). A Median can fail two ways: burn out (Savvy) or lose themselves (Actuality).
Getting out LockedMild mania: the Median claws their own way out. Dire mania: a Soothsayer can reach in and guide the lost back — a vital role in combat and aftercare.
The lasting mark LockedNot a fixed penalty. Sometimes a memory is permanently altered or erased. Sometimes the Median loses several memories of the same emotion — a whole register drained at once (burn through grief, lose grief itself). Sometimes the cost is time — meditation and therapy to heal. The “lose a whole emotion” outcome is a character arc in a mechanic, and a road toward what Silas became.
Silas contrast LockedMania is the inverse of Silas. He is fully Stigmatized yet self-possessed — the one being who doesn’t lose himself. A Median lost to Mania is what it looks like to drown in the very thing Silas mastered.

Recovery & Greying Locked

  • Renewable by default — charge is exhausted, not destroyed. Rest restores it.
  • Greying — drawing too deep, too fast, or too often on one memory keeps the facts but loses the feeling, then eventually the memory itself.
  • Permanent loss — overspending past what rest can recover.
  • Old souls rotate through many memories and last longer; young souls grey fast. This is the stamina curve and the power hierarchy in one mechanic.
Emotional weightMemory is the only inheritance that survives death (if you stay out of the Ley). To spend memory is to spend your immortality.

Prism Walking Locked

Prism Walking is the art of traversing the Aegis Grid by moving through the anima-soaked roots of the Anima Crux that span its expanse. As the name implies, the walker moves through light itself — riding the reflective energies of the Anima that course through the Crux’s roots. The mode is incredibly swift and not easy to use; the Eversouls Prism Walk without any negative side effects, where others risk far more.

Who is trained to Prism Walk Locked · NewAmong the living Services, Veilwalkers and Leywardens are the ones trained to Prism Walk — fitting, as both make their work within the Ley and the Grid. It is a demanding discipline, and part of what sets these Ley-faring Services apart from those who keep to the surface.

Archevims

Auric Remanence — the eighth sense Locked · NewEvery Pneumosian possesses Auric Remanence. It is a literal sense — standing alongside sight, sound, scent, taste, touch, movement, body-limit, and message-interpretation — and it is the soul’s retained reservoir of experience across its previous lives’ aeons: the residue that remains once each life has passed. P.A.R. & B.A.R. Locked · NewThe memories drawn from a soul’s Remanence sort into two tiers. A Protoarch stores up to twelve of a bearer’s Primary memories; a Semiarch, one passing Basic one. (Remanence is the reservoir; memories are what you count and store.)
What an Archevim is Locked · NewAn Archevim is an item that stores Auric Remanence — a physical repository that lets Medians and Generics alike draw Anima from a more consistent, increased, and reliable source of energy. Archevims are natural to the Pneumosian people and carry a wide range of uses. This makes them precious to Generics and Medians alike, and as natural to society as everything else: worn, carried, traded, and relied upon across every walk of Vi’talitas.

Generics & Archevims Locked

Generics lack natural talent and live ordinary lives — but many can deeply connect to Archevims: relics potent with ancient anima that let a Generic use their tiny output at a higher level.

  • Must remain with the user at all times to be effective.
  • Often small items — jewelry, trinkets, accessories — but they can also be built into large, complex machines (see below).
  • Mysterious and dangerous in the wrong hands; an improperly attuned Archevim can overwhelm its user.

The Makers — Aspects & Anitechs Locked

A true Archevim takes two hands to make: one of the Nine Divine Aspects supplies the sacred bond and the rare Matrux; an Anitech then crafts the device. Neither can make one alone — this is the mark of authenticity, and the reason true Archevims are scarce.

The Nine Divine Aspects LockedThe Anima Aspects (§3) both defend the sanctity of the nine Anima Ciphers and dwell within hidden domains at hidden Leypoints inside the Six Veils. They bless genuine Anitechs and provide the extraordinary quality of Matrux required to infuse memory into a crafted item — and they will only train those they consider true Anitechs to do so.
Anitechs — the crafter class LockedSkilled craftsmen responsible for forging the sacred, long-lasting bonds with the Nine Divine Aspects. They take up the profession to become a range of working crafters — but to become a full Anitech, one must travel with the Aspects within their domains for a full aeon (an aeon being a year in Vi’talitas), learning their ways. The apprenticeship transforms a craftsperson into a true, dedicated maker of relics. This training and pilgrimage is undertaken at the Abbeys of the Aspects (§ Organizations) — where mechanical skill and sacred belief are taught together. Stigma & Stigmants

Stigma
The corruption that hollows souls into monsters

Stigma is corrupted Anima — the antithesis of Anima, created by A’thule to combat it. It swallows and corrupts Anima, corroding rifts into the Ley and the realms beyond.

How it forms: Stigma is what greying becomes when taken all the way — anima that has lost connection to a true, lived self. A memory stripped of truth but still carrying charge is a lie that still has power. A soul running on charge with no authentic self behind it is Stigma: power that has forgotten what it was for.

The personal struggle (resisting grey) and the cosmic war (resisting collapse) are the same fight at different scales.

The closed loop Locked Stigma corrodes Aether — and Aether powers both the Aegis and A’thule’s prison. So every soul that falls to Stigma feeds acid into the lock on A’thule’s cage. His weapon, goal, and escape are a single mechanism: spread Stigma.
Treating Stigmatization LockedThe Stigma-sickness from exposure can be treated if caught early — Soothsayers are trained in it — but success is not guaranteed, especially once exposure is prolonged. (Stigmant parts can also treat the stigmatized — see §4.)
Central tragedy — the cure Locked A soul returns from Stigma only by returning to the Ley — total cleansing that erases the self entirely. The disease is fleeing the cycle; the only cure is the cycle. Saving a fallen soul and losing them forever are the same act.

Stigmants Locked

The monstrous manifestations of Stigma that pour from newly opened rifts — the on-the-ground threat beneath A’thule and the Grey Makers. They hold volatile energies, and their parts are valuable: sold for analysis and, darkly, to treat those suffering the effects of being stigmatized — a partial remedy drawn from Stigma itself.

NoteThe Ley’s Leylines and Leypoints also host monstrous entities of both Stigma and Anima, lethal to the untrained.

Stigmant Rankings Locked · New

Stigmants come in varied rankings. Only Gloamite bites can be treated (if caught in time); the rest — Grey-Makers, Greyscales, and Dreads — are beyond cure and must be put down, returning them to the Sea of Lights.

Grey-Makers

The highest Stigmant rank — the stigmatized Eversouls Silas corrupted (A’thule’s Lieutenant tier), and the fallen souls who joined their ranks since. They command lesser Stigmants and “wear familiar faces,” because the first of them were family. (Their role in the antagonist hierarchy is detailed in The Antagonists.) Alongside Greyscales, they are far more difficult to put down than Dreads.

Greyscales
Formerly Medians · common · must be put down

Stigmants who were formerly Medians and have greyed beyond saving. It shows as hardened, monochromatic scales across the skin. Hollowed of their own Auric Remanence, they feed on that of others to replace what they’ve lost. Their Signets have blackened to deep jet and their eyes gone ghostly white. Common, and to be put down — their Fulcrum is often the very place their Signets appear. Unlike Dreads, they retain a measure of self-independence.

Dreads
Corrupted beasts · drones · makers of rifts

Usually beasts of the Pneumaverse, warped and controllable by higher Stigmants (including Grey-Makers). Wild, aimless, and starved for anima with no end to their hunger — more drone than Greyscale, understanding neither purpose nor direction. Their saliva and blood taint the environment, making a bite or scratch dangerous. Considerably warped, losing much of their vibrant coloration and natural disposition. Often the makers of rifts. Easier to kill than Grey-Makers or Greyscales — but no less lost; there is no cure.

Gloamites
Insectoid swarms · treatable if caught in time

Tiny insectoid creatures that move in highly aggressive swarms, spreading Stigma through their bites. Most reside within the Blight and the Anathema, but breakouts have Stigmatized entire towns. They multiply extremely fast if left untended. Unlike the other ranks, Gloamite bites can be treated with proper medication if caught in time.

The Fulcrum Locked

Every Stigmatized being carries a Fulcrum — the anchor-point where the corruption roots into the host’s life-force, the locus holding the twisted form together. It is the Stigma-specific weak point.

A mercy, not a kill LockedDestroy the Fulcrum and the host crumbles — the corrupted body collapses and the soul is released back to the Sea of Lights. A fully Stigmatized creature is already lost to madness and rot; shattering its Fulcrum frees the trapped soul rather than merely slaying a monster. (“Destroy their fulcrums, and let them crumble… these poor things deserve a proper journey back to the Sea of Lights.”) It literalizes the cosmology’s central rule — the only cure for Stigma is returning to the Ley. Finding and reaching a Fulcrum in combat is the core skill of Stigma-fighting work, requiring archevims and a caster who can read where the corruption is anchored. Leywardens and Riftstalkers train for exactly this.

The Institute Lexicon

Lexicon
Standard working-names, by Cipher

The shared vocabulary of Animancy — the formal names the Institutes teach for standard workings. A working-name points at the effect, never at the caster’s private memory, which is precisely why the vocabulary travels: two Medians who have never met can call the same name, fuel it from wholly different memories, and produce the same result.

How a cast takes place — the short form
1
Recall. The Median reaches inward and surfaces a memory through Auric Remanence until its charge is raw. The act is internal — no circle, no implement, no external mechanism.
2
Name the working. Ignition is cognitive: the working-name is fixed in the mind. It may be held there in silence, or spoken. Speech is never required for the magic to function.
3
The signature shows. Colour drains from the body and refocuses into the Signets and eyes, which flood with the Cipher’s colour — sclera and iris both, the pupil left as the only normal point; hair takes the Cipher hue. Entry speed is itself a measure of skill.
4
Release — and hold. The charge fires through the Cipher into the effect. Firing does not drop the Median out of Recall: the state persists and can be spent from repeatedly, limited by Savvy (soul-stamina).
5
Recovery. Colour bleeds back; Signets and eyes cool. Recovery speed reads as power — brief for a master, lingering for the weak or overdrawn. Colour that never fully returns is greying.
Silent, spoken, and what speech buys LockedSilent casting is fully viable and is the ordinary practice of masters — the name is simply thought. Speaking it aloud adds precision and reliability, not force: a silent cast is no weaker, but carries a wider margin of error. Two registers exist for the spoken form — the formal citation Auric Remanence: Loving Embrace, used in training, examination and record; and the field form Loving Embrace, which is what anyone actually says in a fight. Dropping the prefix is fluency, not sloppiness. Because naming aloud costs a beat, it is reserved for workings that warrant it — so among trained Medians, a spoken name reads as escalation.

The Present Age Prelude Locked

For generations, Vi’talitas was an ordinary world — mystical races coexisting, anima-rich but stable. Then its fabric gave way. A massive rift tore open, entangling Vi’talitas with what lay beyond, and Stigma poured through — a corruptive miasma that burns the eyes and mouth, lethal by exposure. Those who gave in became Stigmants, flooding the world as the rift widened, consuming or corrupting people, beasts, plants, even the raw elements.

At that darkest hour, the Medians awakened. Dormant anima rose across every region; past-life memories flooded back into the population, and the Polestar — nucleus of the Ley — awakened, and its people with it. Vi’talitas began returning to what its people had only read of in ancient tomes.

One group of Medians sealed the great breach, saving the reborn world at the cost of their anonymity — remembered only as Type Genesis, the first to show how far Animancy could grow. Their research confirmed Vi’talitas was once Ne’haris. Generations on, this is legend.

Today, Stigmants are a constant threat but no longer apocalyptic. Rifts still open; now there are Medians to subdue them and Generics to support. This is the world the series opens in.

Medians are now essential to Vi’talitas — but it wasn’t always so. For ages they were feared, ridiculed, condemned, and distrusted. Many Generics exploited young Medians through the Undertrade Exchange, a black market that stole newly orphaned Median children with untapped potential; the unluckiest never died clean deaths, and those who fought to live carried an unshakeable will. The arrival of Stigma changed much — reframing Medians from threat to necessity. Even now, an uncontrolled Median is treated as a liability — managed risk, not fear or worship.

The Company of Guardians Locked · New

The primary flagship of the Realmwatcher Service — and the one organization directly employed by the Regency. The Bureau of Bastards Locked · New

An Oathbound talent agency — the flagship for hired expertise of every stripe. Oathbound enlist here to take on odd jobs they alone can complete with fine skill, and the Bureau brokers them out. Code, not chaos — the line from the Undertrade Locked · NewWhat separates the Bureau from the Undertrade is a moral code. Grey, then, but not lawless: the Bureau will take dark work; it will not take any work.

Abbeys of the Aspects Locked · New

Each Abbey blends mechanical training with sacred belief, readying both mind and skill for the work — and each is devoted to a particular Aspect as its focal point (some to Genos, others to the remaining Aspects), so an Abbey’s character reflects the Aspect it serves. A Generic can be taught the sacred work even if they can never cast it. This makes the Abbeys the one training ground where Median and Generic sit side by side as equals in the same calling. Why the two rulers matter

Two scales measure the same passing time: Earth time (the mortal realm’s ruler) and Pneuma time (the Spirit Realm’s). They sit side by side for one purpose — to dramatize how staggeringly old most souls are. A mortal life is brief on the Earth scale; counted in Pneuma units, a soul’s existence stretches almost beyond comprehension.

The felt comparison LockedThe two are not a precise conversion — the equivalences are felt, not arithmetic (in keeping with “the Between”). A mortal century feels like a Pneuma Novam; a mortal hour feels like a Pneuma Aura. The point is the gap between the rulers: a long life in Earth years reads as a vast, deep age in Pneuma units — which is why an old soul carries such weight (its depth feeds its anima pool, instinct, and power; see §4).

The Six Veils Locked

The Sea of Lights is divided into Six Veils — concentric regions of the Spirit Realm. They are not separate afterlives but the terrain of the journey between lives: a soul returns to the Ley, moves through the appropriate Veils, and is drawn back into rebirth (memory wiped, re-emerging anew). A soul’s state at death — peaceful, troubled, unrepentant — shapes which Veils it moves through, whether toward Awakening and Elevation or down into Blight and Anathema. The Veils were once centered on Ne’haris; after the Gloaming — the Ghost Wars’ second battle — and rebirth, Vi’talitas now holds that center.

Technology

Technology
Anima-craft — Archevims, Anitechs, Ether Vessels & HEAT-SR

The crafted side of Animancy: relics, vehicles, and war-machines built from anima and spirit-energy. Much of it traces to I’nácio (remembered as Amoz) — Recalled memory become civilization-shaping craft.

Genre register — Aetherpunk LockedVi'talitas's technology runs on aether / spirit-energy rather than fuel or electricity — Ether Vessels, HEAT-SR machines, and Archevims are powered by soul-stuff. This places the world in the aetherpunk / magitek register (in the spirit of Final Fantasy's soul-powered machinery): magic is the infrastructure. Fantasy-dominant, with technology as an expression of the metaphysics rather than a replacement for it.

Ether Vessels Locked

Spirit-energy vehicles — machines composed of spirit energy that let beings of the Spirit Realm travel across realms for extended periods. They were invented by I’nácio, and as Amoz he remembers the skillset — it has since become widely used across Vi’talitas. A perfect demonstration of the core mechanic: a Recalled memory becoming a civilization-shaping craft. Eversouls thrive within Spirit Space, but outside it their power drains heavily; Ether Vessels solve the travel problem.

How a Vessel works
Ether-Meter
Reads surrounding energy by type, molecular density, gravitational effect, and environmental reaction, then retunes its fuel function to feed on that location’s ether and reach full power. A Vessel runs on the energy of wherever it enters.
Shared source
Built to share its source energy with its pilot — Vessel and pilot power one another.
Centrum
The core, infused with the pilot’s essence — a security bond so only the pilot can run the ship smoothly. A stolen Vessel is a dead one.
Sentient
Like everything born of Ne’haris, Ether Vessels are sentient.

HEAT-SR Machines, the Trauma Contingency Troupe & Sentries Locked

A militarized class of Ether Vessel, also of I’nácio’s / Amoz’s design lineage. HEAT-SR = Hyper Elevated Anima-Transfused Striker Regalia — sentient war-machines, the heaviest end of the craft. The Trauma Contingency Troupe (T.C.T.) is the organization that manages the integrity of the Aegis Grid and contains chaotic anomalies; Leywardens belong to it and operate Ether Vessels — they do not pilot HEAT-SR Machines. The HEAT-SR pilots are Sentries (also a Role open to any Median or Generic; see Roles). When not in their Machines, Sentries mount winged equines with ibex-like horned heads — noble steeds that train alongside them.

I’nácio as originator LockedEther Vessels, the HEAT-SR Machines, and more are I’nácio’s creations, remembered as Amoz and spread into common use. Because I’nácio is the Essence of Ardor — will itself — this engineering reads as a discipline of relentless will made manifest. The T.C.T., in following his designs, in a sense follows him.

The New World — Vi'talitas

Vi’talitas
The New World — present-day geography

Vi'talitas is the world reborn from the Gloaming — the Ghost Wars’ end — that destroyed Ne'haris. Its terrain and structure have changed; this is the present-day world the series takes place in — distinct from the Lost World (§7.7) and the Sea of Lights (Cosmology).

The Three Super-Continents Locked · New

The landmass of Vi’talitas is divided into three pangea-sized super-continents — the rule of three held at the scale of the world itself. Each is the ancestral homeland of one tier of the Pneumosi peoples.

Super-continentPeoplesCharacter
UrbsianHominus, Hybrinus, Neandus (the humanlike peoples)The most developed of the three — running highly on the Anitech craft of Vi’talitas; a land of settlement, industry, and made things.
AssentiaNyph, Feii, Jengun (the truest-spirit peoples)The largest living ecosystem of the world — its beating heart and breathing lungs. Wild, elemental, and closest to the Ley’s living things.

Homeland, not ethnicity Locked · NewThe continents are tier-homelands — geography following the nature of a people (humanlike, truest-spirit, theriomorphic), not their culture or heritage. A Pneumosi’s continent speaks to what kind of being they are, never to who they are as a person; the peoples travel, mix, and settle the world over. (Consistent with the representation framework: identity lives in persons, not in species or place.)
Design payoffThree super-continents mapped one-to-one onto the three Pneumosi tiers keeps the rule of three intact at both scales — three lands, three natures — and gives each tier a coherent ecological and cultural signature (Urbsian = made/Anitech, Assentia = living/elemental, Elysius = elevated/wild) without collapsing the ten distinct peoples into three.

                      ◆The Prophecy — Locked

                      The keystone decision that closes the architecture.

                      The prophecy is not a projection of victory — it is instruction. It tells the siblings how to defeat Silas and A'thule, not that they will. It is conditional, actionable, and must be enacted at a cost.

                      • SilasOnly Silas holds it whole. This is why he hunts them: not fear of fate, but fear of information they could assemble. You can kill the merely fated; you cannot un-know a completed instruction. So it must never complete — kill them before they gather it, across lifetimes. This is why he tracks reincarnation.
                      • All threeThe instruction is distributed across exactly these three souls by design. It can only be assembled by the three people it is written into — which is why A'kana needed all three renewed, why Silas needs all three dead, and why the estranged trio is structurally irreplaceable to one another.
                      • OminReaches the part buried at the soul/Ley boundary — earned through the grief of finally understanding what she heard that night. Not acquired; suffered into.
                      • AmozReaches the part that must be walked toward through danger — learned from the inside, at the risk of becoming what the prophecy warns against.
                      • IonReaches the part that can only be read — accessible precisely because of the wound of being the one Silas killed first.
                      The fragments are not collectible objects — that is the youth-grammar trap in disguise. Each is a truth that costs something to integrate and changes the sibling who receives it. Test: does “finding a part” read like picking up an item, or surviving a revelation? It must always be the second.
                      Assembling the instruction is also the moment they learn what defeating Silas will cost them — method implies price. Completion should not feel like “victory unlocked” but like finally understanding the terms, and the terms should frighten them. Possible: the tragic Silas who believes he is sparing them that cost — the dark mirror of the family's protective-withholding instinct.

                      IThe Comfortable Story

                      Establish the three separated lives, then break the thing that kept them apart.

                      • OminOpen on Omin mid-Leywarden work, alone. Establish her by action and sound — sightless navigation, the trouble that finds her, the weight she carries silently. Not by exposition.
                      • OminThe separation is shown as normal: occasional contact, duties pulling them apart, Amoz's long absences treated as routine. The reader should feel the comfortable lie before it breaks — “he always comes back.”
                      • AmozThe break: something makes this absence different.
                      • Omin · IonOmin and Ion converge — reluctantly, friction of estrangement intact. They do not slot easily back together. Years did something to them.
                      What is the concrete trigger? Something Amoz would never miss? A trail gone cold where it shouldn't? Whatever it is, it should implicate the sisters' complacency — they let the comfortable explanation hold a few months too long. That retroactive guilt is free fuel.
                      End of Movement I — the turnThe decision to actually look for Amoz. Not heroic — guilty.

                      IITwo Searches, One Thread

                      Run the personal search and the cosmic search in parallel — without the characters yet knowing they are the same.

                      • AmozThe trail pulls forward — toward the voice, toward something pre-Ra’quiss. The reader's lore-knowledge primes dread: this voice reads like a Stigma lure even if it isn't.
                      • OminThe search for Amoz reactivates her search for that night. Half-heard memories surface — her mother's voice, her stories of “what their people believed,” the Fonixi / Ra'gnn fragments. She navigates the past as she navigates space: by sound, by what she heard but never saw.
                      • Maisel · IonMaisel's Soothsaying yields pieces she can share only with Ion. Her fractured, formerly-Stigmatized memory delivers exposition compromised — never clean, never fully trustworthy.
                      • Cost plantedFirst terrible Grey Maker encounter seeds here. Establish the price early: to cleanse Stigma is to erase identity. Saving is killing.
                      Amoz's voice — corruption, something the cosmology has taught everyone to fear as corruption, or genuinely ambiguous? Keep it ambiguous to the end of book one. The question is not “what is it” but “is Amoz still Amoz when they reach him.”
                      Maisel's memory loss must have rules. Stigma cleanses by returning soul-identity to the Ley — so her fractures aren't random amnesia, they are partial soul-erasure. What she's lost vs. retained should map the shape of the threat. The absence is information — decide the rule now, or it becomes a writer's escape hatch.
                      End of Movement II — the turnA piece of Amoz's trail and a piece of Omin's memory rhyme — the first hint, felt before understood, that the voice and the night come from the same source.

                      IIIConvergence

                      The longest, hardest movement. Two searches collapse into one. The spine made visible. Build this first — if it holds, the rest follows.

                      • RealizationThe sisters realize they have been investigating the same event from opposite ends of memory: Amoz pulled forward toward a past he can't remember; Omin digging backward into a past she remembers too well.
                      • OminMaiselThe two recovered memories collide — and may not match. Two protectors, same instinct (bury what hurts the family), in direct conflict over whether the truth should surface. Omin may discover Maisel did to her exactly what Omin has always done to Amoz and Ion.
                      • SilasThe realization about Silas arrives as betrayal understood, not mystery solved. He killed A'sai in incubation → Ion is the Unborn because of him. He is upstream of what each of them is. Land it on the body, not the lore.
                      • FragmentsEach sibling's distributed part of the instruction begins to surface — bound to their nature and Service. The prophecy is glimpsed, never whole, because no one but Silas holds it complete.
                      The reunion is not a rescue. Three options for the cliffhanger that defines the series register: (a) found but changed/changing; (b) found but having chosen the voice with open eyes — agent, not victim; (c) not found, but his fate now understood. Recommendation: (b), with the cost ambiguous. It makes him tragic and willed — a mirror of every soul called home to the Ley.
                      End of Movement III — the turnThey reach Amoz, or where Amoz is. The personal wound and the cosmic wound are now one wound — and the siblings can see it.

                      IVThe Question Made Visible

                      Resolve book one's plot (the Amoz search) while opening the series' true subject.

                      • AmozThe immediate thread resolves enough to satisfy book one — at a cost that reframes everything.
                      • The trioThe three end changed in relation to each other. The blood tie survives what time did to them — or survives barely, which is more honest.
                      • The warThe full shape becomes visible: murdered parents, the Unborn, the transformed, the witness, Silas, the corrupted predecessors, the sleeping Eversouls still to find — all one continuous campaign. They now understand the war they're in.
                      • ApertureThe series question — rescue or severance, salvation or long goodbye — is now askable. Book one earns the right to ask it. It does not answer it.
                      Final beat — strongest candidates: (a) the first Grey Maker sibling identified, making “saving is killing” personal and imminent; (c) Silas seen afraid for the first time, confirming he escalates because he is losing. Recommendation: combine (a) and (c) — the personal cost ahead, and the enemy revealed as frightened rather than omnipotent.

                      The Theme That Unifies It

                      Every figure of love or authority in these siblings' lives has decided the truth was too costly to hand over. The series is about what withholding does to the people you withhold from — and whether love that protects by hiding is love, or a quieter kind of harm.

                      Keep the four withholdings irreconcilable — tender, negligent, sacrificial, and monstrous — so the theme stays alive instead of flattening into one note struck four times.

                      OminShields by taking the weight in silence.
                      MaiselShielded by helping them not-remember.
                      SilasWithholds the prophecy — believing, perhaps, he spares them its cost.
                      The parentsDied protecting them — the founding protection-through-removal.

                      ◆Cross-Cutting Pressure Tests

                      1. The collection-quest trap. Freeing Grey Makers / finding Eversouls / stopping Silas is structurally a gather-the-pieces quest — youth grammar. Defense: every objective must hurt because of who these people are. Each Grey Maker freed = family chosen to be erased.
                      2. Silas omniscience. He tracks reincarnation across lifetimes — edges toward a villain who puts the heroes on rails. Defense: he has failed before. Escalation = fear, not godhood. A frightened brother running out of chances is more dangerous and more interesting.
                      3. Maisel as escape hatch. “She misremembered” can paper over anything. Defense: fixed rules for what fractures vs. holds, tied to Stigma-as-soul-erasure. The absence must be information.
                      4. Omin under-rendered. She is the primary but was developed last. Defense: put weight on her on the page. She carries the series because she carries the memory — don't let the primary become the structural orphan of her own book. She orphaned herself (chosen distance) the way the others were orphaned.
                      5. The voice as lure. Your own lore primes the reader to read it as Stigma. Use that — don't resolve it too early. Ambiguity is the tension.
                      6. Three siblings, one unit? Adult trios carry divergence. Make their occasional unity earned against three Services and three lives — not assumed, which would be ensemble-cohort youth grammar leaking back in.

                      ◆Open Questions, Ranked by Draft-Leverage

                      1. Resolved ✓ — keep developingProphecy: instruction, not projection; held whole only by Silas; distributed across the three by nature and Service.
                      2. Highest open leverageThe Movement-I trigger that makes Amoz's absence undeniable — and does it implicate the sisters' complacency?
                      3. HighMaisel's memory rules — what fractures, what holds, and does the pattern map the threat?
                      4. Defines series registerThe Movement-III reunion cliffhanger — found-and-changed, found-and-chosen, or fate-understood?
                      5. Shapes Maisel through book twoDoes Omin read Maisel's withholding as betrayal, recognition, or both?
                      6. Reader sympathy timingIs Omin's distance-as-protection learned early (sympathetic from the start) or discovered (recontextualizing her coldness)?

                      Start with Movement III. If the convergence holds, everything else will.
