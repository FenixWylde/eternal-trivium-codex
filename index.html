    ✦ The Pitch

    Eternal Trivium Locked
    Teaser — one line In a world where magic is memory spent, three orphaned siblings are the reincarnated gods who once died to save it — and they don’t remember why.
    Short pitch — back-cover On the world of Vi’talitas, magic is Animancy — the art of burning your own memories for power, paid not in mana but in the feeling you lose forever. When a tear in reality begins flooding the world with Stigma, a corruption that hollows souls into monsters, the only ones who can seal it are three siblings raised in a home for lost children: Omin, who traded her sight to perceive the unseen; Amoz, who devours the wicked to free their souls; and I’onnie, who hears the dead but is deaf to the living. They are the last incarnations of the fallen god-souls who lost the first war — and to win this one, they must Recall the lives they died living. An epic of memory, mercy, and the cost of power.
    Long pitch — the deeper hook The Great Mother A’kana gave her children the gift of Anima — the power to know and tend a soul — and was betrayed twice: once by her brother A’thule, who forged Stigma to unmake creation, and once from within, when one of her own protectors corrupted the divine Eversouls in a war that broke the world. A’kana spent the last of herself sealing the wound, scattering her surviving children into mortal rebirth with their memories wiped. Generations later, the seal is failing. The three who must mend it — the Elloyd siblings — live as ordinary, distrusted Medians, casters who pull power from their own past lives at the price of greying away, memory by memory, toward the same Stigma they’re sworn to fight. As they uncover what they were, they inherit not just power but guilt: the betrayer was family, the war was kin against kin, and the mercy they deliver to the corrupted — releasing trapped souls back to the cycle — is the same mercy one sister once gave the brother she was forced to strike down. Eternal Trivium braids a coming-of-age, a slow-burn tragedy of reincarnated love, and a cosmology where the only cure for corruption is to return to where you began.
    Title & Branding Locked Series / world title: Eternal Trivium (no “The”). Names the whole structure of reality — the three-Pillar cosmos governed by the Twelve Laws — so every story sits beneath it. Iconic shape (adjective + weighty noun), a canon term rather than a bolted-on name, clean and sayable, and it quietly encodes the “three” that organizes the world. Animancy is the magic within the world (its “Force”), not the title. Three-tier brand: world title → magic system → book subtitles (e.g. Eternal Trivium: Sea of Lights).

    The Core Premise Locked

    The series takes place primarily within the Spiritual Dimension. When a person dies, they choose: return to the mortal realm, or remain in the Spiritual Realm to exist.

    Power in this world is called Animancy. Those who wield it are Medians. Those who cannot are Generics.

    01 Cosmology

    The Cosmology

    The Omneity Locked

    A singular, boundless consciousness — “Fate” or “Destiny” in mortal terms. All and nothing, everywhere and nowhere, beyond beginning or ending. To stand before it is to stand at the edge of all Truths. It understood that true life and true death require true balance — and so divided itself into structure.

    The three Pillars are the structure of reality, each governed by a sibling Paramount.

    The Twelve Eternal Laws Locked

    If the Trivium is the structure, the Twelve Laws are its constitution — the principles that maintain balance across the three dimensions (Mind, Body, Soul), four to each. They are taught as maxims a Median lives and trains by — practical wisdom and cosmic principle at once.

    Who keeps them LockedThe Eversouls upheld the Twelve collectively — not one keeper per Law, but a shared upkeep, like a choir holding a chord. When the Body line fell and the Spirit line broke, no single Law snapped; the whole balance thinned for want of keepers. This is why the present world slips even though the Laws still stand.
    Laws of the Mind
    • The Memory is the Source — Memory is what Anima draws from; the root of all casting.
    • Prepare the Mind, the Body will follow — mental discipline precedes physical power.
    • Emotion does not equate to fact — feeling is not truth; the Neurolist’s discipline and a rebuke of Stigma.
    • Sight is not truth — perception deceives. The Law Silas (Illusion) violates; echoed in the prophecy’s “Believe not what your plain eyes see.”
    Laws of the Body
    • Action is always louder than thought — the Body’s counterweight to the Mind; the vessel speaks.
    • The vessel does not forget — memory lives in flesh as well as mind (pairs with “The Memory is the Source”).
    • Revere your flesh. Let it speak for you — the body as honest expression.
    • What is broken still serves — the flawed vessel is the truest; the doomed-born carry the strongest will. (The protagonists embodied.)
    Laws of the Soul — in A’kana’s own words
    • The Living will Die — no incarnate state is permanent.
    • The Dead will Live — death is not an end; the dead return to living.
    • From Anima we Emerge; To Anima we Return — the conservation Law that Stigma violates by refusing return.
    • So thy Soul will always Endure — the soul persists. Comfort to the faithful, sentence to the damned (Silas endures; A’thule endures).
    The Living will Die. / The Dead will Live. /
    From Anima we Emerge / To Anima we Return /
    So thy Soul will always Endure. This is the Eternal Trivium. — A’kana
    How the Laws function LockedOrdinary Animancy operates within the Laws. Bending or breaking a Law is the source of forbidden power and a doorway for Stigma. The corrupted don’t break assigned Laws — they fail the balance their dimension was meant to keep (the fallen Body line: Desire, Ego, Morality, Integrity unbalanced; Silas violating Sight is not truth).

    The Eternal Cataclysm Locked

    A’thule, ruler of the Mind, came to covet his siblings’ contentment and the Omneity’s authority. He struck first at A’kana, believing her benevolence made her weak. A’thesis intervened and was destroyed defending her; his remains were cast adrift among the Pillars. Enraged, A’kana spent vast energy to tear A’thule from the Astroverse and seal him in a parallel plane just outside the three Pillars — the False Pillars — fortified with Aether.

    Aether & the Aegis Grid Locked

    Aether is the Force of Life — A’kana’s substance. With it she built the Aegis Grid: aetheric currents separating the Pillars so they never directly intersect (which would collapse the balance, one realm engulfing another). The Grid’s powerful rivers form Leylines; their crossings are Leypoints. The Aegis has two jobs: keep the dimensions apart, and keep A’thule sealed.

    Design payoffLeypoints = natural high-stakes zones / dungeons / contested territory, where the membrane between dimensions thins.

    The Ley — the Sea of Lights Locked

    The vast in-between of prismatic light where all soulstuff is collected and transformed into hallowed energy — the rebirth cycle. Souls return here and re-emerge as mortals with only faint echoes of past lives. Anima itself emerges from the Ley.

    A’kana’s Slumber, the Polestar & Ne’haris Locked

    Weakened beyond repair, A’kana created a sanctuary deep in the Pneumaverse. Her incorporeal body became The Polestar — a core of raw, chaotic, radiant aether — which birthed the realm Ne’haris, haven to the Unborn, Unliving, and Unseen, the divine nucleus of the Pneumaverse. There she sleeps, beyond the reach of A’thule’s taint.

    The Aether Crux — The Great Mother Tree Locked

    A’kana’s sleeping essence is also a living World Tree. The Aether Crux (Anima Crux, Tree of Life) is the sentient remaining essence of A’kana of Many Souls — the source of Ne’haris and a system of webbed tendrils spanning the world. Mortals know her as Yggdrasil, the Kabbalah, the World Tree; to Ne’haris she is simply “Great Mother A’kana.” Beyond white — luminance, radiating living light; her bark and leaves make the finest healing remedies known.

    • Beacon of Anima: saturated with soul-energy, she flows Anima out through her roots into worlds beyond — lending influence to all worlds she connects to.
    • The Leylines are her roots: the Ley Lines are the Roots of A’kana, like blood vessels. A new world gets a root that feeds it Anima; the network connects all living worlds to the Soul Space, recycling energy for Balance. Sever a root or kill a world, and it withers back to the Soul Realm.
    • The Aurora: the Crux birthed the Northern Lights — a gateway into the Sea of Lights through which souls traverse.
    • Eight Pillars: rune-carved wards around the tree, representing the pathways of Anima, Light and Dark.
    Why A’thule is limited LockedA’kana is mother of all Anima — every pathway is hers to govern, even the Anima A’thule wields. This is why A’thule can use only a fraction of his full power: her hold over the Anima Domains throttles him. Even sleeping, the Great Mother keeps her hand on the betrayer’s strength.

    Matrux — The Skeleton of Ne’haris Locked

    If Anima is the blood of Ne’haris, Matrux is its skeleton. It is crystallized Animus — a heightened, volatile conduit, “a small battery.” It fuels everything made in Ne’haris and later Vi’talitas.

A recoverable artifact of immense power, waiting — a strong Omin hook.

    The Betrayal of Silas Locked

    In the War for Ne’haris, many Eversouls were Stigmatized — through an inside betrayal by one of their own brothers, Silas. This is the cosmology’s second betrayal-by-kin, rhyming one tier down from A’thule: betrayal-by-family is the recurring engine from gods to children to mortals. It also resolves how Ne’haris was wounded — from within, by one of its protectors. With particular cruelty, Silas killed his sister A’sai in incubation, before she could be born (her status, Unborn, is literal) — yet she arrived anyway as I’onnie.

    The three (four) fates LockedThe roster encodes how an Eversoul can fall: living (untouched), stigmatized (fell completely, lost — self dissolved), corrupted (Silas’s distinct state: fully Stigmatized yet self-possessed — the singular being who kept his will through total corruption), and Neversoul (fell but escaped full Stigmatization). The present-day Elloyds are Neversouls — they did not die in the War. What they escaped may still lie dormant within them.

    The Unresolved War Locked as ambiguous

Three siblings — the prophesied Elloyds (see §6) — orchestrate its renewal toward the future of the Trivium.

    03 Anima & Animancy

    The Engine, the Nine Brands & Soul Access

    Anima — the Source Locked

    Anima is inherent and tied to the intensity of the soul. One soul lives many lives; each adds experience. The more lives lived, the more powerful the soul and the greater its base anima pool. Young souls have little; some have none.

    Origin of Anima LockedAnima is the “magic” substance of the world — a natural element permeating the entire Pneumaverse, the stuff the Aegis Grid is made of and that A’kana’s aura radiates. It originated from A’kana as a gift: the means to navigate the existence and makeup of a Soul. So Animancy is not stolen or transgressive — it is a birthright of the Pneumaverse, the Great Mother’s endowment. (A sacred gift, not a theft.)
    The Eye of the Aegis LockedAnima is produced at the Eye of the Aegis — the center and core of Vi’talitas, where the Great Mother Tree stands. From this core, Anima wells up and pushes outward through A’kana’s roots (the Leylines) into the Aegis Grid and the worlds beyond. Because Vi’talitas is Ne’haris, the Eye is the single point where the Tree, the world’s core, and the wellspring of all Anima coincide — the beating heart of the cosmos’s soul-economy.

    Animate vs. Inanimate Souls Locked

    Who can wield Animancy comes down to a soul’s Anima frequency.

    SoulNature
    Animate (Medians)Natural capacity to reincarnate without earning it — theory: they healed their souls last life, renewing their Anima connection. Fluent in Animancy after transition.
    Inanimate (Generics)Low Anima frequency; cannot reincarnate naturally — theory: generational trauma or distancing life-choices frayed the connection. Most work to cleanse the taint for a future chance.
    ImplicationThe Median/Generic divide isn’t arbitrary talent — it’s a soul-state. A Generic isn’t lesser, but estranged from Anima (often through inherited or chosen wounds) and able to heal back toward it.

    Common vs. Elevated Animancy Locked

    • Common Animancy (“Acts of Animancy”): basic daily uses — ecosystem, agriculture, technology, travel, sustainability. Untrained inanimates are limited to this and lean on anima-infused materials (crystals, bones, runes, machinery — i.e. Archevims). The workaday magic that runs civilization.
    • Elevated Animancy: Animancy as an art, not a tool. No infused materials — only natural ability, channeled through the self or an ancient relic. The Median’s domain (and the rare trained Generic’s aspiration).
    Mastery is hardEven an animate soul must discover its Anima Brand (the nine Aspect-Archetypes) and learn to wield it fully. Inanimates who can’t cast directly are offered other roles working anima-infused materials — many build content lives this way.

    Casting — Recall & Release Locked

    A Median casts by Recalling a memory (surfacing it through auric-memory until its charge is raw) and Releasing that charge through a Brand, which shapes it into an effect — simply Recall → Release. Memory intensity equals cast intensity. The memory is the fuel; the Brand is the lens (any memory can fuel any Brand). The cost is felt, not numeric: Releasing a memory means temporarily losing access to its emotional charge. The act is fundamentally internal — the Median reaches inward rather than relying on an external mechanism.

    Auric-Memory — the Eighth Sense LockedEvery race on Vi’talitas possesses auric-memory as a literal sense — alongside sight, sound, scent, taste, touch, movement, body-limit, and message-interpretation. It is the ability to identify memories from past lives and call upon them for present use. This is the sensory faculty that underlies all memory-casting: Animancy is auric-memory, trained and spent. The Feii are its master-teachers, and their connection to the Nyph ties the elemental peoples to this lineage.
    The visible signature — the Signets LockedCasting is subtractive, and the signature is the cost made visible. Where other powers add light to the world, Animancy briefly takes light from the caster. Every Median bears Signets — Anima-marks placed on the body at awakening, their location fixed by Brand so placement announces a Median’s discipline (Origin on the face, Qhaos on the back, etc.; see the Brand Resonance table). Aether is the exception, surfacing where the Median channels best. They lie dormant until casting.
    The Cast Cycle
    Recall → Release → Recovery — one visible cascade of color leaving and returning.
    Recall
    Color drains from the body and refocuses into the Signets and eyes, which ignite in the Brand’s color. The eyes take the Brand hue regardless of natural color: sclera and iris both flood with it, leaving only the pupil as a point of normal black or white (by race). The Median’s hair also shifts to the Brand’s color for the cast. No white remains in the eye — a striking, faintly inhuman look.
    Release
    The gathered charge fires through the Brand into the effect.
    Recovery
    Color bleeds back into the body; Signets and eyes cool to normal. Recovery speed = power — brief for a master, lingering for the weak or overdrawn.
    Two tells, read at different moments LockedLoud-novice / quiet-master reads during Release: a novice leaks, the Signet-mark blazing wild; a master bleeds almost nothing, the mark a near-silent whisper. Drain-and-recovery reads after: a master is quick in the return; the weak stay greyed. The axes aren’t redundant — one reads in the firing, one in the aftermath. A true master is quiet in both.
    Greying made visible LockedBecause casting is “color leaves, then returns,” a greyed memory is one whose color never fully comes back. Overspend, and a Signet stays dull, an eye stays washed-out — a permanent, readable scar. A veteran’s Signets map their history of loss; a sharp onlooker can read how much a Median has spent of themselves off their own body.
    Omin — the signature in full Locked
    The complete drain-and-Signet cascade is precisely her tell.

    The Aetherseer casts with the full cycle — drain, Signets and eyes alight in Aether’s gold, recovery. The poignancy of how a sightless caster’s lit eyes read — what it means to blaze with a light she cannot see — is reserved for her character pass.

    The Sequence — The Recall State Locked

    Casting is not a single instantaneous act but an entered state that can then be spent from repeatedly — like a fighter’s powered-up form.

    Ignite → Sustain → Release (repeatable)
    Recall is ignition
    Entering the charged state can be near-instant for the skilled — a master drops into Recall in a heartbeat; a novice is slow. Speed of entry is itself a measure of skill.
    A sustained form
    Once in the Recall State, the Median holds it — it persists for as long as their soul-stamina allows.
    Release doesn’t end it
    Firing a Release does not drop them out of Recall. They stay in the form and can Release as often as required while it holds — a barrage, not a single shot.
    Savvy is the fuel
    What limits the form is Savvy (soul-stamina). Each Release and moment held draws on it; when it runs low the form dulls and collapses. (See Vitalitas → Savvy, Game System.)
    Endurance is trained
    Holding the form longer and recovering Savvy faster is a discipline every Median must build — even masters worked to extend it. Soul age helps; endurance is earned.
    Why it matters LockedThis turns casting from a per-shot transaction into a resource-managed state — a Median chooses when to enter the form, then fights from inside it until their Savvy gives out. Combat gains a rhythm: ignite → barrage → burn down → recover. Soul-stamina, not per-cast cost, is the real ceiling on power in a fight.

    The Memory — Emotion Shapes the Cast Locked

    A memory is not fuel of one flat kind. Every memory carries an emotion or sensation, and that feeling shapes the cast. Intensity of memory still sets intensity of effect — but the character of the feeling steers how that power expresses.

    • Tendencies, not a table. A memory invoking anger tends to increase intensity (force); one invoking calm tends to increase precision (control). Other feelings carry their own leanings — but two angry memories never produce identical spells. (Real rules, human cost, never arithmetic.)
    • Casting is autobiographical. The feeling is the steering, so a Median’s magic reflects who they have been. A caster who fights well on rage is a different person from one who fights on grief or joy.
    • Vs. Vitalitas: attributes are the baseline; emotion is the in-the-moment modulation. Tenacity sets how hard you can hit; casting on anger is how you lean into it now. They stack.
    The core tension LockedThe most emotionally intense memories are the most powerful fuel and the most dangerous (see Mania). The best Medians aren’t those with the strongest memories — they’re those who can ride strong memories without drowning.

    The Mania — Losing Oneself to the Memory Locked

    Because casting means feeling, a Median can Recall too much — pulling a memory so deep it stops being a tool and becomes a place. They tip into Mania (distinct from the Berserker Role). It has two faces, and the memory’s emotion decides which strikes:

    The two faces of Mania
    Outward — loss of control
    Memories that throw the cast outward (rage → force) throw the Median outward: the Brand fires wild and uncommanded, no aim, no off-switch — a danger to allies and self. The body acts; the steering is gone.
    Inward — loss of self
    Memories that turn the cast inward (grief, calm) pull the Median inward: consciousness submerges into the memory, leaving a coma-like state — present in body, absent in mind, trapped reliving it. They must find their way out.
    The two bulwarks LockedTwo Vitalitas attributes guard the two faces. Savvy holds the form (run out, the Recall State collapses); Actuality — perceive and assert what is real — anchors the self (run low, the memory swallows you). A Median can fail two ways: burn out (Savvy) or lose themselves (Actuality).
    Getting out LockedMild mania: the Median claws their own way out. Dire mania: a Soothsayer can reach in and guide the lost back — a vital role in combat and aftercare.
    The lasting mark LockedNot a fixed penalty. Sometimes a memory is permanently altered or erased. Sometimes the Median loses several memories of the same emotion — a whole register drained at once (burn through grief, lose grief itself). Sometimes the cost is time — meditation and therapy to heal. The “lose a whole emotion” outcome is a character arc in a mechanic, and a road toward what Silas became.
    Silas contrast LockedMania is the inverse of Silas. He is fully Stigmatized yet self-possessed — the one being who doesn’t lose himself. A Median lost to Mania is what it looks like to drown in the very thing Silas mastered.

    Recovery & Greying Locked

    • Renewable by default — charge is exhausted, not destroyed. Rest restores it.
    • Greying — drawing too deep, too fast, or too often on one memory keeps the facts but loses the feeling, then eventually the memory itself.
    • Permanent loss — overspending past what rest can recover.
    • Old souls rotate through many memories and last longer; young souls grey fast. This is the stamina curve and the power hierarchy in one mechanic.
    Emotional weightMemory is the only inheritance that survives death (if you stay out of the Ley). To spend memory is to spend your immortality.

    04 Stigma & Stigmants

    The Corruption

    Stigma is corrupted Anima — the antithesis of Anima, created by A’thule to combat it. It swallows and corrupts Anima, corroding rifts into the Ley and the realms beyond.

    How it forms: Stigma is what greying becomes when taken all the way — anima that has lost connection to a true, lived self. A memory stripped of truth but still carrying charge is a lie that still has power. A soul running on charge with no authentic self behind it is Stigma: power that has forgotten what it was for.

    AnimaStigma
    Individuation — separate souls, separate dimensions, the cycle.Dissolution — boundaries failing, everything merging toward A’thule’s unity.

    The personal struggle (resisting grey) and the cosmic war (resisting collapse) are the same fight at different scales.

    The closed loop Locked Stigma corrodes Aether — and Aether powers both the Aegis and A’thule’s prison. So every soul that falls to Stigma feeds acid into the lock on A’thule’s cage. His weapon, goal, and escape are a single mechanism: spread Stigma.
    Treating Stigmatization LockedThe Stigma-sickness from exposure can be treated if caught early — Soothsayers are trained in it — but success is not guaranteed, especially once exposure is prolonged. (Stigmant parts can also treat the stigmatized — see §4.)
    Central tragedy — the cure Locked A soul returns from Stigma only by returning to the Ley — total cleansing that erases the self entirely. The disease is fleeing the cycle; the only cure is the cycle. Saving a fallen soul and losing them forever are the same act.

    05 The World on the Ground

    Society on Vi’talitas

    Medians in Society Locked

    Medians are now essential to Vi’talitas — but it wasn’t always so. For ages they were feared, ridiculed, condemned, and distrusted. Many Generics exploited young Medians through the Undertrade Exchange, a black market that stole newly orphaned Median children with untapped potential; the unluckiest never died clean deaths, and those who fought to live carried an unshakeable will. The arrival of Stigma changed much — reframing Medians from threat to necessity. Even now, an uncontrolled Median is treated as a liability — managed risk, not fear or worship.

    HookThe Undertrade Exchange — trafficking in stolen Median children — is a ready source of villains, backstory wounds, and rescue/origin arcs.

    The Asylum for Aberrant Aptitude — “Triple-A” Locked

    The most proficient institution for studying Anima — and more than that, a home for Medians left without one. Students are studied ethically and taught to enter society safely and openly, without hiding their gifts. Its mission: guide as many young Medians as possible to awaken their best self.

    Prime Soothsayer Maisel Wone — “Mama Maisel” has run the Triple-A for as long as most remember, never quite seeming to age. A born Vitalist, her power is a mystery; few dare test how fearsome a protector she can be. Warm to all who seek to train, fiercely protective of every student.

    Maisel’s secret LockedMaisel is the Eversoul Mulu’lizi, Silver Essence of Integrity (the Body line, not the Restoration line her wards belong to). She was the caretaker older sibling — tending D’vita and I’nácio, carrying guilt over losing A’sai (the baby Silas killed in incubation). Then she fell — fully stigmatized. Of all the fallen, D’vita could save only her: using Vaine’s discovery of Silas’s scheme, D’vita cleansed her and returned her to the Ley, where she reincarnated as Maisel. The carer became the one who needed carrying; saving her meant erasing her.
    What Maisel knows now LockedOver time she has pieced much of her past back together. She knows the Eversouls existed, that she is one, and that her remaining kin are hidden to keep them safe. Much remains theory — she couldn’t find every answer — but she understands that it falls to the three siblings to answer the rest. A knowing guide who withholds and waits, not an oblivious one.
    The instinct survived the erasure LockedMaisel now raises the reincarnations of the very siblings she once tended — D’vita → Omin, I’nácio → Amoz, and the baby she couldn’t save, A’sai → I’onnie. Mulu’lizi’s defining trait — care, guilt, protection of her siblings — outlasted the Ley’s wipe and reassembled as “Mama Maisel.” What she “recognized” in their souls wasn’t facts — it was her own children to protect.
    The buried debtMaisel does not know any of this. She raises Omin without knowing Omin is D’vita — the sister who saved her life by erasing her. If Omin recovers her past, she’d find that the woman who raised her is the sister she once sent to die-and-return. A reunion waiting in the wings.
    Design payoffThe Triple-A is a natural player hub / tutorial home / faction base; Mama Maisel is a mentor whose unaging Vitalist nature is its own mystery hook.

    Generics & Archevims Locked

    Generics lack natural talent and live ordinary lives — but many can deeply connect to Archevims: relics potent with ancient anima that let a Generic use their tiny output at a higher level.

    • Must remain with the user at all times to be effective.
    • Most are small, wearable items — jewelry, trinkets, accessories.
    • A true Archevim takes two hands to make: an Aspect supplies the blessing and the rare Matrux drawn from its own form; an Anitech (a skilled mortal artificer) then crafts the device. Neither can make one alone — the mark of authenticity, and the reason they’re scarce (each traces back to a pilgrimage to an Aspect, not just a forge).
    • Mysterious and dangerous in the wrong hands; an improperly attuned Archevim can overwhelm its user.
    The Nine Divine Aspects LockedNow named (§3): the Anima Aspects, guardian-teachers of the nine Brands who dwell in Leypoints. They bless genuine Archevims and provide the rare body-Matrux they’re built from, and they train the worthy.
    Anitechs — the crafter class LockedMortal artificers who actually build Archevims from an Aspect’s blessing and body-Matrux. A real profession with workshops and guilds; without an Aspect’s materials, an Anitech makes only lesser anima-infused goods, never a true Archevim. To become a full Anitech, one must travel with the Aspects within their domains for a full year, learning their ways — the apprenticeship that turns a craftsperson into a true maker of relics.
    Design payoffGeneric + Archevims = a viable gear-dependent playstyle. Attunement risk = a built-in equip cost/curse mechanic.
    Stigma-charged Archevims LockedAn Archevim can run on Stigma-charge — but it isn’t built to, and breaks down from the strain over time. A villain’s bargain with a built-in cost: cheaper, stronger, quietly damning, self-destroying.

    Ether Vessels Locked

    Spirit-energy vehicles — machines composed of spirit energy that let beings of the Spirit Realm travel across realms for extended periods. They were invented by I’nácio, and as Amoz he remembers the skillset — it has since become widely used across Vi’talitas. A perfect demonstration of the core mechanic: a Recalled memory becoming a civilization-shaping craft. Eversouls thrive within Spirit Space, but outside it their power drains heavily; Ether Vessels solve the travel problem.

    How a Vessel works
    Ether-Meter
    Reads surrounding energy by type, molecular density, gravitational effect, and environmental reaction, then retunes its fuel function to feed on that location’s ether and reach full power. A Vessel runs on the energy of wherever it enters.
    Shared source
    Built to share its source energy with its pilot — Vessel and pilot power one another.
    Centrum
    The core, infused with the pilot’s essence — a security bond so only the pilot can run the ship smoothly. A stolen Vessel is a dead one.
    Sentient
    Like everything born of Ne’haris, Ether Vessels are sentient.

    HEAT-SR Machines, the Trauma Contingency Troupe & Sentries Locked

    A militarized class of Ether Vessel, also of I’nácio’s / Amoz’s design lineage. HEAT-SR = Hyper Elevated Anima-Transfused Striker Regalia — sentient war-machines, the heaviest end of the craft. The Trauma Contingency Troupe (T.C.T.) is the organization that manages the integrity of the Aegis Grid and contains chaotic anomalies; Leywardens belong to it and operate Ether Vessels — they do not pilot HEAT-SR Machines. The HEAT-SR pilots are Sentries (also a Role open to any Median or Generic; see Roles). When not in their Machines, Sentries mount winged equines with ibex-like horned heads — noble steeds that train alongside them.

    I’nácio as originator LockedEther Vessels, the HEAT-SR Machines, and more are I’nácio’s creations, remembered as Amoz and spread into common use. Because I’nácio is the Essence of Ardor — will itself — this engineering reads as a discipline of relentless will made manifest. The T.C.T., in following his designs, in a sense follows him.

    The Ragnn & Fonixi — The Sky Peoples Locked

    The Elloyds’ parent-races: two allied, matriarchal sky peoples descended from related deities — the Perennial Dragon and the Celestial Phoenix, who eternally chase one another.

    Ragnn — Dragon-descended
    Subgroups: Flammas, Aquis, Tellus, Aeris.

    Huge (7–9 ft), brute-strong, dense-boned; wings, horns, prehensile tail, rippling scales, hearty appetites. Proud, battle-ready — “brains as well as brawn.” Cannot self-reincarnate; they welcome death and ready for the next lifecycle.

    Fonixi — Phoenix-descended
    Subgroups: Coastal, Marshland, Prairie, Forest.

    Tall (6–7 ft), lean, delicate; powerful wings, taloned feet, feathered limbs, excellent vision; formidable aerial hunters. Can reincarnate themselves when the body fails — but only via a Fonixi High Priestess ritual — making them the second longest-lived race.

    Lineage resonance LockedThe siblings are a Ragnn-mother / Fonixi-father pairing (traditionally doomed). Their parent-deities, the Dragon & Phoenix, are Stasyss & Dynaass — the Empyrons held to be A’kana’s grandchildren. So the Elloyds descend, mortally, from the same divine line their Eversoul nature comes from.

    Stigmants Locked

    The monstrous manifestations of Stigma that pour from newly opened rifts — the on-the-ground threat beneath A’thule and the Grey Makers. They hold volatile energies, and their parts are valuable: sold for analysis and, darkly, to treat those suffering the effects of being stigmatized — a partial remedy drawn from Stigma itself.

    NoteThe Ley’s Leylines and Leypoints also host monstrous entities of both Stigma and Anima, lethal to the untrained.

    The Fulcrum Locked

    Every Stigmatized being carries a Fulcrum — the anchor-point where the corruption roots into the host’s life-force, the locus holding the twisted form together. It is the Stigma-specific weak point.

    A mercy, not a kill LockedDestroy the Fulcrum and the host crumbles — the corrupted body collapses and the soul is released back to the Sea of Lights. A fully Stigmatized creature is already lost to madness and rot; shattering its Fulcrum frees the trapped soul rather than merely slaying a monster. (“Destroy their fulcrums, and let them crumble… these poor things deserve a proper journey back to the Sea of Lights.”) It literalizes the cosmology’s central rule — the only cure for Stigma is returning to the Ley. Finding and reaching a Fulcrum in combat is the core skill of Stigma-fighting work, requiring archevims and a caster who can read where the corruption is anchored. Leywardens and Riftstalkers train for exactly this.

    The Empyrons — Beasts of Ne’haris Locked

The breadth of Elan types is an open catalogue — a natural home for companion/mount design.

This fits the soul-intensity premise: a soul intense enough carries enough Anima to awaken and infuse the vessel; Generics lack that intensity. The name Median comes from their eventual ability to cross the rifts into the Sea of Lights — they are “in the middle” of worlds.

    06 The Protagonists

    The Elloyd Siblings Locked

    Three orphaned siblings, stolen as infants for the blood in their veins, raised by Mama Maisel at the Triple-A. All three are Vitalists whose power was awakened by trauma rather than natural growth. All reach Tier 0 by graduation, and all take the surname Xero.

    The “Three” of prophecy LockedThey are the three foretold in the Whispers of the Mother — “Await the three who stir the Unborn, Unliving and Unseen.” Not mirrors of the Paramounts; they are Neversouls — Restoration Eversouls who fell but escaped full Stigmatization, now living mortal lives on Vi’talitas. Each embodies one of the three Pneumosi classes — Omin = Unseen, Amoz = Unliving, I’onnie = Unborn (§2) — because that is what they were made to be.

    Heritage & Lineage Locked

    • Born of a Ragnn mother and Fonixi father — peoples linked by belief in Stasyss the Immutable Dragon and Dynaass the Perennial Phoenix, held to be grandchildren of A’kana, who first took the form of the primeval beast, the first Empyron.
    • Empyrons protected Ne’haris and its secrets; since its transition, none are seen except within the Ley.

    7.5 Where the Series Opens

    The Present Age — The Inciting Event Locked

    For generations, Vi’talitas was an ordinary world — mystical races coexisting, anima-rich but stable. Then its fabric gave way. A massive rift tore open, entangling Vi’talitas with what lay beyond, and Stigma poured through — a corruptive miasma that burns the eyes and mouth, lethal by exposure. Those who gave in became Stigmants, flooding the world as the rift widened, consuming or corrupting people, beasts, plants, even the raw elements.

    At that darkest hour, the Medians awakened. Dormant anima rose across every region; past-life memories flooded back into the population, and the Polestar — nucleus of the Ley — awakened, and its people with it. Vi’talitas began returning to what its people had only read of in ancient tomes.

    Time & Soul-Age Locked

    Two scales measure the same passing time: Earth time (the mortal realm’s ruler) and Pneuma time (the Spirit Realm’s). They sit side by side for one purpose — to dramatize how staggeringly old most souls are. A mortal life is brief on the Earth scale; counted in Pneuma units, a soul’s existence stretches almost beyond comprehension.

    The felt comparison LockedThe two are not a precise conversion — the equivalences are felt, not arithmetic (in keeping with “the Between”). A mortal century feels like a Pneuma Novam; a mortal hour feels like a Pneuma Aura. The point is the gap between the rulers: a long life in Earth years reads as a vast, deep age in Pneuma units — which is why an old soul carries such weight (its depth feeds its anima pool, instinct, and power; see §4).
