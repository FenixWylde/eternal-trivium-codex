## 0. Title & Branding **[LOCKED]**
- **Series / world title:** **Eternal Trivium** (no "The"). Names the whole structure of reality — the three-Pillar cosmos governed by the Twelve Laws — so every story sits beneath it. Iconic shape (adjective + weighty noun, like *Final Fantasy*); canon term, not bolted on; clean and sayable; secretly encodes the "three" that organizes the world.
- **Magic system:** **Animancy** — the power *within* the world (its "Force"), not the title.
- **Three-tier brand:** world title (*Eternal Trivium*) → magic system (*Animancy*) → individual book subtitles beneath the umbrella (e.g. *Eternal Trivium: Sea of Lights*).
- **Note:** the word "Trivium" is shared by a metal band (no conflict — common-root words aren't protectable and the full title differs); the phrase "Eternal Trivium" is unclaimed.

## 1. The Core Premise

The series takes place primarily within the **Spiritual Dimension**. When a person dies, they face a choice: return to the mortal realm, or remain in the Spiritual Realm to exist.

Power in this world is called **Animancy**. Those who can wield it are **Medians**. Those who cannot are **Generics**.

## 2. The Cosmology (Top-Down)

### The Omneity **[LOCKED]**
A singular, boundless consciousness — "Fate" or "Destiny" in mortal terms. All and nothing, everywhere and nowhere, beyond beginning or ending. To stand before it is to stand at the edge of all Truths. It understood that true life and true death require **true balance**, and so it divided itself into structure.

### The Tri-Dimensional Pillars (The Trivium) **[LOCKED]**
The three Pillars are the **structure** of reality. Each was governed by a sibling Paramount:

| Pillar | Domain | Paramount | Epithet | Fate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Astroverse** | Mind | **A'thule** | of Many Faces · Dark Matter | Betrayer; sealed |
| **Materiaverse** | Body | **A'thesis** | of Many Eyes · Matter | Slain defending A'kana |
| **Pneumaverse** | Spirit | **A'kana** | of Many Souls · Anti-Matter / The Great Mother | Sealed A'thule; now sleeps |

> **The matter-trinity [LOCKED]:** The Paramounts are formed of the three substances of existence — A'thule = **Dark Matter** (Mind, the unseen pull), A'thesis = **Matter** (Body), A'kana = **Anti-Matter** (Soul, the inversion-force that can seal and cleanse).

> **[LOCKED] Domain mapping:** A'thule = **Mind**, A'thesis = **Body**, A'kana = **Spirit**. This is foundational — the nine aspects of Animancy descend from these three domains.

### The Twelve Eternal Laws **[LOCKED]**
If the Trivium is the **structure**, the Twelve Eternal Laws are its **constitution** — the principles that maintain **balance across the three dimensions** (Mind, Body, Soul). They divide **four to each dimension**, mirroring the threeness woven through the whole cosmos. The Laws are taught as maxims a Median lives and trains by — practical wisdom and cosmic principle at once.

> **Who keeps them [LOCKED]:** The **Eversouls** maintained the Twelve Laws **collectively** — not one keeper per Law, but a shared upkeep, like a choir holding a chord. When the Body line fell entirely and the Spirit line broke into Neversouls, no single Law snapped; the *whole balance thinned* for want of keepers. This is why the present world slips — Stigma spreading, the Aegis weakening — even though the Laws still stand.

**Laws of the Mind**
1. **The Memory is the Source** — Memory is what Anima draws from; the root of all casting.
2. **Prepare the Mind, the Body will follow** — Mental discipline precedes physical power (the Mind's primacy).
3. **Emotion does not equate to fact** — Feeling is not truth; the Neurolist's core discipline, and a quiet rebuke of Stigma (feeling unmoored from truth).
4. **Sight is not truth** — Perception can deceive. The Law Silas (Essence of Illusion) exists to violate; echoed in the prophecy's *"Believe not what your plain eyes see."*

**Laws of the Body**
1. **Action is always louder than thought** — The Body's counterweight to the Mind's primacy; the vessel *speaks*.
2. **The vessel does not forget** — Memory lives in flesh as well as mind — trauma, instinct, muscle-memory. (Pairs with "The Memory is the Source.")
3. **Revere your flesh. Let it speak for you** — The body as honest expression.
4. **What is broken still serves** — The flawed vessel is the truest; the doomed-born and the sense-sacrificed carry the strongest will. (The protagonists embodied.)

**Laws of the Soul** *(in A'kana's own words — the Pneumaverse is her domain)*
1. **The Living will Die** — No incarnate state is permanent.
2. **The Dead will Live** — Death is not an end; the dead return to living.
3. **From Anima we Emerge; To Anima we Return** — Anima is both source and destination; the conservation Law that **Stigma violates** by refusing return.
4. **So thy Soul will always Endure** — Across the turning, the soul persists. The guarantee — *comfort to the faithful, sentence to the damned* (Silas endures; A'thule endures).

> **A'kana's Verse [LOCKED]:** The Soul Laws are drawn from the Great Mother's scripture —
> *"The Living will Die. / The Dead will Live. / From Anima we Emerge / To Anima we Return / So thy Soul will always Endure. / This is the Eternal Trivium."* — A'kana
> *(This verse names the series.)*

> **How the Laws function [LOCKED]:** Ordinary Animancy operates *within* the Laws. **Bending or breaking a Law** is the source of the most dangerous, forbidden power — and a doorway through which Stigma enters. The corrupted don't break *assigned* Laws; they fail the **balance their dimension was meant to keep** (e.g. the fallen Body line — Desire, Ego, Morality, Integrity unbalanced; Silas violating *Sight is not truth*).

### The Eternal Cataclysm **[LOCKED]**
A'thule, ruler of the Mind, grew to covet his siblings' contentment and the Omneity's authority. He struck first at A'kana, believing her benevolence made her weak. A'thesis intervened and was destroyed defending her; his remains were cast adrift among the Pillars. Enraged, A'kana spent a vast portion of her own energy to tear A'thule from the Astroverse and seal him in a parallel plane just outside the three Pillars — the **False Pillars** — fortified with **Aether**.

### Aether & The Aegis Grid **[LOCKED]**
**Aether** is the Force of Life — A'kana's substance. With it she built the **Aegis Grid**: a vast network of aetheric currents separating the Pillars so they never directly intersect (which would collapse the balance, one realm engulfing another). The Grid's powerful rivers form an ethereal "highway" — Leylines — and where they cross are **Leypoints**. The Aegis has two jobs: keep the dimensions apart, and **keep A'thule sealed**.

> **Design payoff:** Leypoints = natural high-stakes zones / dungeons / contested territory, where the membrane between dimensions thins.

### The Ley / Sea of Lights **[LOCKED]**
The vast in-between of prismatic light where all soulstuff is collected and transformed into hallowed energy. This is the **rebirth cycle**. Souls return here and re-emerge as mortals with only faint echoes of past lives. **Anima itself emerges from the Ley.**

### A'kana's Slumber, The Polestar & Ne'haris **[LOCKED]**
Weakened beyond repair, A'kana created a sanctuary deep in the Pneumaverse. Her incorporeal body became **The Polestar** — a core of raw, chaotic, radiant aether — which birthed the realm **Ne'haris**, haven to the *Unborn, Unliving, and Unseen*, the divine nucleus of the Pneumaverse. There she sleeps, beyond the reach of A'thule's taint.

### The Aether Crux — The Great Mother Tree **[LOCKED]**
A'kana's sleeping essence is not only the Polestar — it is a **living World Tree.** The **Aether Crux** (the **Anima Crux**, the **Tree of Life**) is the **sentient remaining essence of A'kana of Many Souls**, the source and foundation of Ne'haris and an immense system of **webbed tendrils** spanning the world. Mortals across the cosmos know her by many names — *Yggdrasil, the Kabbalah, the World Tree* — but to Ne'haris she is simply **"Great Mother A'kana."**

- **Appearance:** beyond white — **luminance**, radiating bright living light. To stand in her presence is **warmth and security.** Her bark and leaves carry her essence and make the **finest healing remedies** known.
- **The beacon of Anima:** the Crux is the **primary beacon for soul-energy (Anima)**, saturated through her whole being; her power flows out through her **roots into worlds beyond.** She lends influence not just to Ne'haris but to **all worlds she is connected to.**
- **The Leylines are her roots [LOCKED — major]:** the Ley Lines mortals speak of are the **Roots of A'kana**, functioning like **blood vessels.** When a **new world is born**, a great root entwines into it and **feeds it Anima** so it can flourish; this bio-neurological network of aether **connects all living worlds to the Soul Space**, recycling energy in the name of **Balance.** If a root is severed or a world dies, that root **withers and returns to the Soul Realm to be reprocessed.**
- **The Aurora:** the Crux is where the **Aurora Borealis / Northern Lights** were born — seen from many worlds, considered a **gateway into the Sea of Lights** through which souls traverse.
- **The Eight Pillars:** surrounding the tree are **eight massive rune-carved pillars** of warding, representing the **pathways of Anima, Light and Dark.**

> **Why A'thule is limited [LOCKED — resolves a big question]:** Because A'kana is the **mother of *all* Anima**, every pathway of it is hers to govern — **even the Anima A'thule and his following wield in the Outer Realms.** This is why **A'thule can only use a *fraction* of his full power:** A'kana's hold over the Anima Domains throttles him. The Great Mother, even sleeping, keeps her hand on the betrayer's strength.

### Animatrux (Animus / Matrux) — The Skeleton of Ne'haris **[LOCKED]**
If Anima is the **blood** of Ne'haris, **Animatrux** is its **skeletal frame.** It is **crystallized Animus** — a heightened energy conduit, "a small, volatile battery." It fuels everything made in Ne'haris and, later, on Vi'talitas.

> *"As Animus be the Life Blood / And Matrux be the Growing Bone / May Sea Lights be the Shifting Breath / That Guides you Home."*

- **It is alive:** Animatrux is a **living crystal**, almost always *active* — which is why it's so volatile. To **wear** it safely demands extraordinary control of one's Spiritual Energy, or the bearer may **combust.** Only **specialized, trained individuals** handle it.
- **It is not truly a mineral [LOCKED — major]:** it is a **resin** — the **hardened sap of the Anima Crux (the Tree of Life).** So it **grows and spreads as far as the Tree's roots reach** — and those roots run **through the Aegis Grid.**
- **Mining:** the **Singing Caves** are the only place it can be mined **safely.** Other deposits exist across Ne'haris but are unsanctioned and unneeded.
- **How A'thule got some:** because the roots thread the Aegis, A'thule obtained a **limited but potent** quantity — but **Animatrux cannot survive the Outer Sectors**; it dies and withers there. With steady access to Ne'haris he could have held it for eons.
- **Attunement & imprinting [LOCKED]:** Animatrux **imprints on its user** — once attuned, it becomes an **extension of the soul**, letting them call on **Anima Spells.**
- **Matrux-Altera [LOCKED]:** as the taint spread, **dark/corrupted versions** of Animatrux began to form — **Matrux-Altera.** (The normal mineral's appearance is **white fire opal**; Altera is its corrupted variant.)

> **D'vita's Ring [LOCKED]:** Every Eversoul bore an Animatrux **ring** of white-fire-opal appearance. **D'vita's** was especially potent (her Aetheric affinity) — it was **where most of her power resided**, a literal extension of her soul. When she transitioned into the long Sleeping, **her ring was left within her memory.** A bearer's ring, on transition, **stays tethered to the moon in the Spirit Realm.** *(A recoverable artifact of immense power, waiting — a strong Omin hook.)*

### The Eversouls — A'kana's Children **[LOCKED]**
To defend the Pneumaverse, **A'kana created the Eversouls** — her children, instrumental to the founding of **Ne'haris** and the protectors of its deepest secrets. They are a distinct tier of being: **below the Paramounts but far above ordinary souls.**

> **Why this deepens the system [LOCKED]:** The Eversouls are the Spirit lineage made manifest. This is the *true* reason Soul Restoration / **Vitalists** are the rarest, most sacred Type — a true Vitalist may be a **Neversoul** (a fallen Eversoul living a mortal life) or their lineage. The rarity now has a bloodline, not just a probability. It likely also relates to the **Empyrons** — A'kana's own beast-form lineage — though the exact relationship is **[OPEN]**.

> **Tier of being (now four levels):** Omneity → Paramounts (A'thule / A'thesis / A'kana) → **Eversouls** (A'kana's children) → ordinary souls (Medians, Generics).

### The Betrayal of Silas **[LOCKED]**
In the War for Ne'haris, **many Eversouls were Stigmatized** — and the cause was an **inside betrayal by one of their own brothers, Silas.** This is the cosmology's *second* betrayal-by-kin, rhyming one tier down from A'thule's: betrayal-by-family is the recurring engine from gods to their children to mortals. It also resolves *how* Ne'haris was wounded — **from within, by one of its protectors.**

Silas's betrayal struck the Restoration line with particular cruelty: he **killed his sister A'sai *in incubation*, before she could be born** (her status, *Unborn*, is literal). The gentlest of the three he tried hardest to erase — yet she arrived anyway, as **I'onnie.**

> **The three fates [LOCKED]:** The roster encodes how an Eversoul can fall (see the Children of A'kana table below). **Stigmatized** = fell completely (lost). **Corrupted** = Silas's distinct state as the betrayer. **Neversoul** = *fell but escaped full Stigmatization* — touched by the corruption yet not consumed. The present-day Elloyds are **Neversouls**, not reincarnations: they did **not** die in the War. What they escaped may still lie dormant within them.
> **[OPEN] / strong HOOK:** Is **Silas now among (or the chief of) the Grey Makers**? It would explain how they wear familiar faces and whisper with intimacy — one of them *was* family. See Section 9.

## 3. The Magic System: Animancy

### Anima — The Source **[LOCKED]**
Anima is **inherent** and tied to the **intensity of the soul**. One soul lives many lives; each life adds experience. The more lives lived, the more powerful the soul, and the greater its base **anima pool**. Young souls have little; some have none.

> **Origin of Anima [LOCKED]:** Anima is the **"magic" substance of the world** — a **natural element permeating the entire Pneumaverse**, the stuff the **Aegis Grid** is made of and that **A'kana's aura radiates.** It originated **from A'kana as a gift:** the means to **navigate the existence and makeup of a Soul.** So Animancy is not stolen or transgressive — it is a *birthright of the Pneumaverse*, the Great Mother's endowment for understanding and tending the soul. *(This sets the world's moral relationship to its power: a sacred gift, not a theft.)*

### Animate vs. Inanimate Souls **[LOCKED]**
Why some souls wield Animancy and others can't comes down to a soul's **Anima frequency** — its closeness of connection to Anima.

- **Animate souls (Medians)** carry a **natural capacity to reincarnate** *without* having to actively earn it. The theory: in their most recent life they **healed their souls**, renewing their connection to Anima — so after transition the soul is "animate," fluent in Animancy.
- **Inanimate souls (Generics)** bear a **low Anima frequency** and **cannot reincarnate naturally.** The theory: the soul was **cursed by generational trauma**, or made **distancing choices** in its last life — either way its connection to Anima frayed, leaving it "inanimate" after transition. Most inanimate souls **work to cleanse this taint** for a future chance at reincarnation.

> **Implication:** the Median/Generic divide isn't arbitrary talent — it's a *soul-state.* A Generic is not "lesser," but **estranged from Anima**, often through inherited or chosen wounds, and capable of healing back toward it. This reframes the social hierarchy (Section 7) as a matter of soul-health, not worth.

### Common vs. Elevated Animancy **[LOCKED]**
Anima flows through all the Pillars, but it is **not naturally usable** by the inexperienced. Most residents are attuned only to **basic** applications:

- **Common Animancy ("Acts of Animancy")** — basic, practical uses woven into daily life: **ecosystem, agriculture, technology, travel, sustainability.** Untrained inanimates are limited to this, and to do even this at fuller strength they **rely on anima-infused materials** — crystals, bones, runes, machinery (i.e. **Archevims**). This is the workaday magic that keeps civilization running.
- **Elevated Animancy** — Animancy as an **art rather than a tool.** It requires pushing skill past common limits and needs **no** infused materials — only the wielder's **natural ability**, channeled through themselves or an **ancient relic.** This is the Median's domain (and the rare trained Generic's aspiration).

> **Mastery is hard:** even an animate soul must discover its **Anima Aspect** (the nine Architect-Archetypes: Creation, Divine, Wild, Eldritch, Grymm, Chaos, Ardor, Arcane, Aether) and learn to wield that form fully. Inanimates who can't perform Animancy directly are, in fairness, **offered other roles** — working anima-infused materials and objects — and many build content lives this way.

### Casting — Recall & Release **[LOCKED]**
A Median casts by **Recalling** a memory (surfacing it through auric-memory until its charge is raw and available) and **Releasing** that charge through an Aspect, which shapes it into an effect. The two-beat act is simply **Recall → Release.** Memory intensity = cast intensity: a trivial memory → a trivial effect; an identity-defining memory → an enormous one. The memory is the **fuel**; the Aspect is the **lens** (any memory can fuel any Aspect — the Aspect shapes the output).

- The cost is **felt**, not numeric — Releasing a memory means temporarily losing access to its emotional charge.
- The act is fundamentally **internal** — the Median reaches *inward* to Recall and Release, rather than relying on an external mechanism.

> **The visible signature [LOCKED — skill ladder]:** Power is read by **how loud or controlled the casting "signature" is.** A **novice leaks** — the charge sprays out messily, so the Aspect's mark blazes wild and bright. A **master is precise** — almost nothing bleeds, so the mark is a near-silent whisper. *You know a master because their casting has almost no tell.* (The exact nature of the signature — and how the nine Aspect sigils figure into the visible act — is being developed in a dedicated pass; what's locked here is the **loud-novice / quiet-master axis**.)

### Recovery & Greying **[LOCKED]**
- **Renewable by default:** memory charge is *exhausted*, not destroyed. **Rest restores it.**
- **Greying (the danger):** drawing too deep, too fast, or too often on one memory risks *greying* it — keeping the facts but losing the feeling, then eventually the memory itself.
- Overspending past the point rest can recover = **permanent loss**.
- **Old souls** rotate through many memories and fight longer without greying; **young souls** grey fast. → This is the stamina curve AND the power hierarchy in one mechanic.

> **Emotional weight:** memory is the only inheritance that survives death (if you stay out of the Ley). To spend memory is to spend your immortality.

Each group descends from one Paramount / dimension. The three groups represent three **relationships to memory**.

### Aspect Sigils — Visual Identity **[LOCKED]**
Each Aspect has a canonical **brand symbol** and color (see `Anima_Brand_Symbols.png`). The palette follows the dimensional coding — Mind cool, Body hot/elemental, Soul celestial:

### Mind Preservation (Astroverse / A'thule) — memory as *Information*
*Low cost, high precision. Utility, control, reconnaissance. The everyday workhorses.*
- **Origin** — Manifestation & Conjuration
- **Hallow** — Inspiration & Divination
- **Feii** — Manipulation & Abjuration

### Vessel Deconstruction (Materiaverse / A'thesis) — memory as *Force*
*High cost, high impact. Damage and alteration. Greys fastest.*
- **Wylde** — Elemental & Transmutation
- **Grymm** — Necromation & Abolition
- **Qhaos** — Devastation & Evocation

### Soul Restoration (Pneumaverse / A'kana) — memory as *Self*
*Rare. Acts inward on the caster's own soul. Sustain, efficiency, defense. The "immune system" against Stigma.*
- **Arcane** — Psionic Proficiency → recover/restore greying memory; access locked past lives.
- **Ardor** — Willful Proficiency → overcharge memory; hold the self together past the breaking point.
- **Aether** — Spiritual Proficiency (rarest) → touch the boundaries between souls/lives; share pools, anchor dying souls, perceive other souls.

> **Why Soul Restoration is rare [LOCKED]:** the six outward aspects spend memory *onto the world*; Soul Restoration spends it *onto the soul itself* — surgery on the thing doing the surgery. It requires an unusually old or integrated soul (rarity = soul-age = power = narrative weight, automatically).

### Corruption Vulnerability by Group **[LOCKED implication]**
- **Mind Preservation** users are closest to A'thule's domain → best at *detecting* Stigma AND most *tempted* by it. (Early-warning system + highest-risk defectors.)
- **Vessel Deconstruction** users fall through *exhaustion/recklessness* (a cleaner danger). Lineage of the martyr A'thesis.
- **Soul Restoration** users are the immune system. Lineage of the Great Mother.
> Moral textures: **Body = martyr's inheritance**, **Mind = betrayer's shadow**, **Spirit = mother's protection**.

### Median Types (Archetypes) **[LOCKED]**
A Median is classified into one of three **Types** — the proficiency identity resonating with one aspect-group. A Median finds their Type as they mature.

**Neurolist** (Mind Preservation — Origin / Hallow / Feii). Advanced capability through the mind; *"if you can think it, it can happen,"* taken literally. Emotion is strongly tethered to their psionic power, so **constant emotional control is key**. The **second most common** Type. They fill education, governance, technology, and biological wellness.

**Physiolist** (Vessel Deconstruction — Wylde / Grymm / Qhaos). Enhanced physical prowess — strength, speed, sight, sometimes extra limbs. Their gifts often **manifest visibly in the body**, making them the most distinctive to the eye. The **most common** Type, with a direct connection to Vi'talitas. They fill agriculture, manufacturing, infrastructure, and environmental stewardship.

**Vitalist** (Soul Restoration — Arcane / Ardor / Aether). Deep connection to the soul; the **rarest** Type. Their command of Anima is uncannily natural, as mysterious as Stigmants and the rifts. They commune with the Living and the Unliving alike and attune directly to the material of the Ley.
> **The Vitalist's Sacrifice [LOCKED]:** because of their sheer natural anima, a Vitalist **retracts one of the five senses** (sight, sound, touch, taste, or scent) as they grow in skill. Greater attunement may cost **more than one sense**. This is the personal price of the rarest gift — and a vivid character-design lever. In exchange, Vitalists have **flexibility of technique** the other Types lack by design.

> **Note on "born" Medians:** A Median's essence is *infused* with Anima, normally awakening in adolescence (timing varies by race). This reconciles with the soul-intensity premise: a soul old/intense enough carries enough Anima for it to **awaken and infuse the vessel**; Generics lack that intensity. The name *Median* comes from their eventual ability to travel the rifts into the Sea of Lights — they are *"in the middle"* of worlds.
> **Alternate awakening [LOCKED]:** Under the right combination of circumstances, **severe trauma can awaken a Median's power early** (even in infancy), bypassing the normal adolescent timing. The Elloyd siblings awakened this way.

### Median Tiers (Power Level) **[LOCKED]**
Independent of Type, every Median has a **Tier** — their level of power, expertise, and versatility.

- **Tier 1 = most exceptional; Tier 9 = least.** A lower number means the Median is *more attuned to their anima's source*; a higher number means further from attunement, regardless of effort.
- A Median typically **peaks at a Tier and stays there for life.**
- **Tier 0 — exceedingly rare.** Can fluctuate freely across the Tier 1–9 range at will, with little trouble — *but at a high cost.*

> **Tier-as-surname [LOCKED]:** Many Medians adopt their Tier number as a surname in place of a birth name — first name + spelled number, styled as the individual prefers. E.g., *Ora Sevn, Tobin Nyne, Malia Aate, Calvis Tu.*
> **RPG payoff:** Tier is a clean, legible power-rating system; Tier 0 is a natural "main character / endgame" rarity with a built-in drawback to balance it.

## 5. Born vs. Forged (Soul Restoration access) **[LOCKED]**

Soul Restoration is **mostly birthright, rarely earned**.
- **The Born** — ancient/integrated souls who do it intuitively. High efficiency, lower control, often don't know *how* they do it. Power feels instinctive and a little wild.
- **The Forged** — rare young/mid souls who earned it through brutal trials. Low efficiency, high mastery ceiling, understand the mechanism intimately (they had to dismantle themselves to learn).

> **Two axes, not one [LOCKED clarification]:** Keep *origin* and *awakening* distinct.
> - **Origin** (innate vs. earned): are you a *born* Vitalist, or a non-Vitalist who *forged* the ability through trials? The Elloyds are **born**.
> - **Awakening trigger** (natural vs. trauma): does the gift surface through normal adolescent growth, or is it forced open early by trauma? The Elloyds awakened through **trauma**.
> So a character can be **born + trauma-awakened** (the Elloyds), **born + naturally-awakened** (e.g. Maisel, presumably), or **forged** (earned from outside). Training/honing at the Asylum is a *separate* third step that applies to all of them.

> **[OPEN] / [HOOK]** The *forging* trials imply an **institution or sacred site** that runs them — gatekeeping and politics. (Note: the Triple-A trains and hones, but it's unconfirmed whether it also runs the rare **forging** of non-born Vitalists, or whether that happens elsewhere.) Most who attempt forging fail (grey/unravel), keeping the population small.
> **RPG payoff:** Origin + awakening = two character-creation dials, not one.

## 6. Stigma — The Corruption **[LOCKED]**

Stigma is **corrupted Anima** — the antithesis of Anima, created by A'thule to combat it. It can swallow and corrupt Anima, corroding rifts into the Ley and the realms beyond.

**How it forms (grows from the existing mechanic):** Stigma is what **greying becomes when taken all the way** — anima that has lost connection to a true, lived self. A memory stripped of truth but still carrying charge is *a lie that still has power*. A soul running on charge with no authentic self behind it is Stigma: **power that has forgotten what it was for.**

- Thematically: **Anima = individuation** (separate souls, separate dimensions, the cycle); **Stigma = dissolution** (boundaries failing, everything merging toward A'thule's desired unity).
- The personal struggle (resisting grey) and the cosmic war (resisting collapse) are **the same fight at different scales**.

### The Closed Loop **[LOCKED]**
Stigma corrodes **Aether** — and Aether is what powers *both* the Aegis *and* A'thule's prison. So **every soul that falls to Stigma feeds acid into the lock on A'thule's cage.** His weapon, his goal, and his escape are a single mechanism: *spread Stigma.*

### Treating Stigmatization **[LOCKED]**
**Stigmatization** (the Stigma-sickness from exposure) **can be treated if caught early.** **Soothsayers** are trained in this treatment — but success is **not guaranteed**, especially once exposure is prolonged. *(Note: Stigmant parts can also be used to treat the stigmatized — see Stigmants, Section 7.)*

### Cure / Redemption **[LOCKED]**
A soul can only return from Stigma by **returning to the Ley** — total cleansing, but it **erases the self entirely** (full memory wipe). The disease is *fleeing the cycle*; the only cure is *the cycle*.
> **Central tragedy:** saving a fallen soul and losing them forever are *the same act*. Redemption = death of identity. (A series-defining tension.)

## 7. Society on Vi'talitas

### Medians in Society **[LOCKED]**
Medians are now **essential to Vi'talitas — but it wasn't always so.** For ages they were feared, ridiculed, condemned, and distrusted. Not all used their gifts for good; many Generics exploited young Medians for profit through the **Undertrade Exchange** — a black market that stole newly orphaned Median children with untapped potential. The unluckiest never died clean deaths; those who fought to live carried an unshakeable will in their eyes. **The arrival of Stigma changed much** — it reframed Medians from threat to necessity.

After training, a Median chooses a **Path** that aligns with them (see Paths below). Even now, Medians are treated less with love or hate than as **liabilities** if they fail to control their Animancy — managed risk, not fear or worship.

> **The Undertrade Exchange [LOCKED] [HOOK]** — a black-market trade in stolen Median children. A ready source of villains, backstory wounds, and rescue/origin arcs.

### The Asylum for Aberrant Aptitude ("Triple-A") **[LOCKED]**
The most proficient of the institutions that study Anima. More than a research body, the **Triple-A is a home** for Medians left without one because their gifts went unchecked. Students are studied ethically and taught to **enter society safely and openly**, without hiding their gifts. Its mission: guide as many young Medians as possible to *awaken their best self.*

**Prime Soothsayer Maisel Wone — "Mama Maisel"** runs the Triple-A and has for as long as most remember, **never quite seeming to age.** A **born Vitalist**, her power is a mystery to most; few have dared test how fearsome a protector and educator she can be. Warm to all who seek to train, fiercely protective of every student past and present.

> **Maisel's secret [LOCKED]:** Maisel is the Eversoul **Mulu'lizi, Silver Essence of Integrity** — of the **Deconstruction (Body)** line. She was the **caretaker older sibling**, tending **D'vita and I'nácio** attentively, and she carried **personal guilt over losing A'sai** (the baby Silas killed in incubation). Then she herself fell — **fully stigmatized.** Of *all* the fallen, **D'vita could save only her:** with knowledge from **Vaine's** discovery of Silas's corruption-scheme, D'vita **cleansed Mulu'lizi and returned her to the Ley**, where she reincarnated as **Maisel.** The carer became the one who needed carrying; saving her meant *erasing* her.

> **What Maisel knows now [LOCKED]:** Over time, Maisel has **pieced much of her past back together.** She now knows the **Eversouls once existed**, that she is one, and that her **remaining kin have been hidden to keep them safe** (the living Mind line; the sleeping I'nácio; the shielded core). She knows a great deal — but much remains **theory**, because she has not been able to find every answer herself. Crucially, she understands that **it falls to the three siblings to answer the rest.** *(So Maisel is a knowing guide, not an oblivious one — she withholds and waits more than she's ignorant.)*
> **The instinct survived the erasure [LOCKED]:** in the present, Maisel runs an asylum-home for lost children and **raises the reincarnations of the very siblings she once tended** — D'vita → Omin, I'nácio → Amoz, and the baby she couldn't save, A'sai → I'onnie. Mulu'lizi's defining trait — *care, guilt, protection of her siblings* — outlasted the Ley's wipe and reassembled itself as "Mama Maisel." This is what she "recognized" in their souls: not facts, but **her own children to protect.**
> **The buried debt [HOOK]:** Maisel does **not** know any of this. She raises Omin without knowing Omin *is* D'vita — the sister who saved her life by erasing her. If Omin ever recovers her past, she'd discover that the woman who raised her is the sister she once sent to die-and-return. An extraordinary reunion waiting in the wings.
> **RPG/narrative payoff:** the Triple-A is a natural **player hub / tutorial home / faction base**; Mama Maisel is a mentor figure whose unaging Vitalist nature is its own mystery hook.

### Generics & Archevims **[LOCKED]**
**Generics** lack natural talent and live ordinary lives — but many can deeply connect to **Archevims**: relics potent with **ancient anima** that let a Generic use their tiny anima output at a higher level.

- An Archevim **must remain with the user at all times** to be effective.
- Most are **small, wearable items** — jewelry, trinkets, accessories.
- Every true Archevim was **crafted by one of the Nine Divine Architects of Anima** (the Anima Architects, Section 2) — the defining mark of authenticity.
- Their power is **mysterious and dangerous in the wrong hands**; an improperly attuned Archevim can **overwhelm its user** (a real risk for Generics).

> **The Nine Divine Architects [LOCKED]** — now named (Section 2): the **Anima Architects**, guardian-teachers of the nine Aspects who dwell in Leypoints. They are both the **makers of genuine Archevims** and the masters who train the worthy.
> **RPG payoff:** Generic + Archevims = a viable gear-dependent playstyle native to the world. A clever, well-equipped Generic can outfight a lazy old Median. Attunement risk = a built-in equip cost/curse mechanic.
> **Stigma-charged Archevims [LOCKED]:** An Archevim *can* run on **Stigma-charge** — but it isn't built to, and it will **break down from the strain over time.** A villain's bargain with a built-in cost: cheaper, stronger, quietly damning, and self-destroying. (Moral economy intact.)

### Pneumosi **[LOCKED]**
Those who live in the Spiritual Realm (also called **Leykin**); they have the most understanding of the Aegis/Leyline structure. Their full four-class order (Unborn / Unseen / Unliving / Unmortal) is detailed in **Section 2**.
> **[OPEN]** Do the Pneumosi *know* they live in A'kana's body/renewal? Is the true history known, forbidden, or forgotten (surviving as garbled religion)?

### The Ragnn & Fonixi — The Sky Peoples **[LOCKED]**
The Elloyds' parent-races: two allied, **matriarchal** humanoid peoples who share the skies, descended from related deities — the **Perennial Dragon** and the **Eternal/Celestial Phoenix**, who eternally chase one another.

**Fonixi** (subgroups: Coastal, Marshland, Prairie, Forest) — descendants of the **Celestial Phoenix.** Tall (6–7 ft), lean, delicate-featured, with powerful **wings**, **taloned bird-of-prey feet**, and feathered limbs. Excellent vision; formidable aerial hunters. They nest in high peaks, trees, or hillside caves. Devout to their deity; they can **reincarnate themselves** when the body fails — but **only** via a ritual performed by a **Fonixi High Priestess** — making them the **second longest-lived race** on Vi'talitas.

**Ragnn** (subgroups: Flammas, Aquis, Tellus, Aeris) — descendants of the **Perennial Dragon.** Huge (7–9 ft), brute-strong, dense-boned, with **wings**, ornate **horns**, a **prehensile tail**, rippling **scales**, and hearty appetites. Proud and battle-ready by nature — *"brains as well as brawn."* Also matriarchal. Unlike the Fonixi, they **cannot** self-reincarnate; instead they **welcome death** and ready themselves for the next lifecycle.

> **Lineage resonance [LOCKED]:** The siblings are a **Ragnn-mother / Fonixi-father** pairing (traditionally doomed offspring). Their parent-deities, the Dragon and Phoenix, are **Stasyss and Dynaass** — the Empyrons held to be A'kana's grandchildren (see Section 8). So the Elloyds descend, mortally, from the same divine line their Eversoul nature comes from.

### The Empyrons — Beasts of Ne'haris **[LOCKED]**
Enormous beasts resembling a cross between **lizard, avian, canine, and feline** — usually winged, with very long lifespans. **Age is read by their number of wing-sets** (more wings = older). They are the **parent species of Dragons, Phoenixes, Sphinxes, and Gryphons.**

- **Origin & society:** native to **Ne'haris**; they live in **Fleets** with close familial bonds and **heightened intelligence.**
- **Nature:** they **thrive on Anima.** Peaceful but **not pacifists** — fiercely **territorial and protective of kin.** They stay **neutral in politics** and avoid others' affairs. They are the **beast of representation for Ne'haris**, found in fair abundance at every corner of the world.
- **The tragedy [HOOK]:** they are **hunted for their bodies.** Empyr **scales, claws, horns, and teeth are riddled with pure Aether Anima** — more valuable, and **more powerful, than Anima crystals themselves.** *(A built-in poaching economy and a moral wound in the world — harvesting the sacred for power.)*

> **Connects to:** Stasyss (the Immutable Dragon) and Dynaass (the Perennial Phoenix) are Empyrons / A'kana's grandchildren and the Ragnn & Fonixi progenitors (Section 7). A'kana herself first took the form of the **first Empyron.**
### The Mortal Races of Vi'talitas **[LOCKED]**
All races share one defining trait: **auric-memory** (see below). The detailed peoples:

**Hominus** (Urban & Rural) — bipedal humanoids resembling Homo sapiens, but distinct: they have **no lungs** (breathing through air-filled tubes branched along the skeleton, regulated via the nasal cavity), bodies **built for adaptive survival** (stronger bone, tissue, skin, and senses than a human), 4–6 ft tall.

**Hybrinus** (Feline, Canine, Avian) — a genus of the Hominus family who can **shift into a specific animal form at will**, with animal features showing even in humanoid form. Said to be **"inhabited by two spirits"** — their Hominus self and an **ancient beast** — sharing the beast's traits and personality; their auric-memory carries the **beast's lifecycles.**

**Nyph** (Wood, River, Sky, Ash) — elemental humanoids who **manipulate the primordial element they hail from.** Related to the **Feii** (environment strongly shapes their communities). Generally pacifist but fierce defenders of homeland; 4–5 ft; renowned craftsmen, architects, farmers, landscapers — honest, loyal, curious.

**Feii** (Solar & Lunar) — enigmatic winged insectoids (max ~4 ft, able to **shrink to 5.5–10 inches**), with vast aristocratic societies, long lifespans, and a deep natural grasp of Anima — **especially the Laws of Existence.** Possibly the **oldest race** on Vi'talitas; **keepers and teachers of auric-memory**, consulted to train its highest use.

**Neandus** (Dryland & Highland) — an **archaic ancestor of the Hominus.** *(Detail pending.)*

**Khimeran** (Stone, Crystal, Metal), **Jengun** (Freshwater & Saltwater), **Centauri** (Plains, Forest, Mountain) — *(names and domains established; full profiles pending.)*

> **Auric-Memory — the Eighth Sense [LOCKED — important]:** Every race on Vi'talitas possesses **auric-memory** as a literal *sense* — alongside sight, sound, scent, taste, touch, movement, body-limit, and message-interpretation. Auric-memory is the ability to **identify memories from past lives and call upon them for present use.** *This is the sensory faculty that underlies all memory-casting* (Section 3): Animancy is auric-memory, *trained and spent.* The **Feii** are its master-teachers; the **Feii** connection to the Nyph ties the elemental peoples to this lineage.


### Stigmants **[LOCKED]**
The monstrous manifestations of Stigma that **pour from newly opened rifts** — the moment-to-moment, on-the-ground threat (as opposed to A'thule and the Grey Makers above them). They hold **volatile energies**:
- Their **parts are valuable** — sold for scientific analysis and, notably, to **treat those suffering the effects of being stigmatized** (a Stigma-sickness exists, and a partial remedy comes *from Stigma itself* — a dark, useful irony).
- Hunting them is a paid profession (see **Path of the Riftstalker**).
> The Ley's Leylines and Leypoints also host monstrous entities of **both Stigma and Anima**, dangerous to the untrained.

## 7.5 Paths — The Median Professions **[LOCKED]**

After training, Medians take a **Path**: a vocation that protects or supports society in ways Generics cannot. These are your **class/career archetypes** for the RPG.

**Lawseeker** — Scholars of Anima, Stigma, Medians, and Stigmants. They unravel the mysteries of the Ley, Vi'talitas, the Eternal Laws, and the Tri-Dimensions. *(The in-world researchers — your lore-keepers and quest-givers; thematically tied to the Twelve Laws.)*

**Soothsayer** — Educators and guides who teach younger Medians toward their full potential. *(Mama Maisel's Path. Note: "this does not always mean for the better" — room for corrupt mentors.)*

**Oathbound** — Hired for jobs that don't revolve around the Ley. Once a job is taken, an **Oath binds them to complete it** — by whatever means necessary. Ranges from respected labor and social roles to the unlawful. *(Your mercenaries, fixers, and morally grey contractors.)*

**Realmwatcher ("Watcher")** — Seal rifts and raise protective barriers for nearby citizens. Specialists in **defensive animancy** and **sacred geometric soulcrafts** that can shield others. The **largest and most common** Path, organized by region. To be a Watcher is a great honor. *(Your frontline defenders / guardians.)*

**Riftstalker** — Hunt and capture **Stigmants**, well compensated for kills and harvested parts. Work as mercenaries, escorts, or extermination units when a rift opens. *(Your monster-hunters / bounty class.)*

**Lightwalker** — Cross rifts to **explore Leypoints** and retrieve time-lost relics and anima-infused resources (including **archevims**), which sustain Vi'talitas through commerce. Explorers and adventurers — driven by respect for the Ley *and* by profit or personal desire. *(Your dungeon-delvers / treasure-hunters.)*

**Leywarden** — Require special training to **travel the Ley itself**: investigate it, keep it functioning, and slay any threat to its balance. They (and Lightwalkers) can cross into **other spiritual realms beyond Vi'talitas** and return to the living realm — mostly — unscathed. Their respect for the Ley is absolute; they see what others never could. **Vitalist Medians almost always have the natural capability to become Leywardens.** *(Your elite, lore-deep endgame class.)*

> **Design note:** Within the Ley, *time folds on itself and the veil is nonexistent*; the unfortified can **unravel from existence**. This is why Lightwalker/Leywarden access is gated — a natural difficulty wall and a source of cosmic-horror set-pieces.

## 8. The Protagonists — The Elloyd Siblings **[LOCKED]**

Three orphaned siblings, stolen as infants for the blood in their veins, raised by **Mama Maisel** at the Triple-A. All three are **Vitalists** whose power was **awakened by trauma** rather than natural adolescent growth (a known phenomenon under the right combination of circumstances). All three reach **Tier 0** by graduation — and all take the surname **Xero**.

> **The "Three" of prophecy [LOCKED]:** The Elloyds are the three foretold in *the Whispers of the Mother* — *"Await the three who stir the Unborn, Unliving and Unseen."* They are **not** mirrors of the Paramounts; they are **Neversouls** — Restoration Eversouls who fell but escaped full Stigmatization, now living mortal lives on Vi'talitas (the world reborn from A'kana's core). Each **embodies one of the three Pneumosi classes** — **Omin = Unseen, Amoz = Unliving, I'onnie = Unborn** (see Section 2) — because *that is what they were made to be.*

### Heritage & Lineage **[LOCKED]**
- Born of a **Ragnn** mother and **Fonixi** father — peoples linked by shared belief in **Stasyss the Immutable Dragon** and **Dynaass the Perennial Phoenix**, held to be grandchildren of **A'kana**, who first took the form of the primeval beast, the **first Empyron**.
- **Empyrons** were the protectors of Ne'haris and its secrets; since Ne'haris's transition, none are seen except by those traveling within the Ley. *(Full species profile in Section 7.)*
- Ragnn/Fonixi pairings are traditionally **doomed** — offspring rarely survive or are born with severe handicaps. The siblings fit this fate: each is "handicapped" by a sense or burden tied to their gift.
- **Maisel recognized their souls** as her own kin — they are **Eversoul siblings** of the Restoration line (see the roster in Section 2). The Elloyds are the **Neversoul** forms of three fallen Restoration children: **D'vita → Omin, I'nácio → Amoz, A'sai → I'onnie.** They did not die in the War; they fell and escaped full Stigmatization, and now live as mortal Medians.
- They consciously remember their Eversoul lives only as **faint echoes and dreams** — the Ley wiped the rest. *(Omin's quest to learn "what happened to Ne'haris" is secretly an act of self-recovery: she is investigating an event she survived but cannot remember.)*

### Omin (Ominira) Xero — Primary Protagonist · the Aetherseer **[LOCKED]**
*Eversoul form: **D'vita**, Unbroken Essence of Aether (Neversoul — reborn).*
*Eldest. Defensive, nurturing, matured before her time. Vitalist · **Aether** Anima · **Tier 0** — the only of her kind.*
- **Sense traded:** sight. She **sees the Unborn** via Aether and can manipulate others' Aether by linking it to her own — "knowing someone to their core" at a glance, sometimes seeing past lives they themselves can't.
- **Unique among all Vitalists:** the only one in the Asylum's history with **no single spiritual guide** — she communes with *many* beings beyond the veil instead of being tethered to one.
- **Tier 0 fluctuation** lets her stay **undetectable** to Medians and Stigmants — a combat edge and an asset as a **Leywarden**.
- **Cost:** wakeful-dream events, depression, anxiety (normal Vitalist developmental strain, but acute in her). Her power often pushes her over the edge.

## 9. The Antagonist Architecture **[LOCKED]**

A'thule's forces form a **chain of command** — a military hierarchy descending from the sealed god to the individual soul:

1. **A'thule — Father of Corruption** (the sovereign). Sealed Mind-Paramount "of Many Faces." Cannot act directly; commands through his elite. His nature is seduction, not force — he *convinces*.
2. **The Falseones** (the Generals). A'thule's **children made of pure Stigma** — the *anti-Eversouls*, each built to combat a specific Eversoul. His named high command, grown in the False Pillars — the "Cold-Bloods": Advisor Null "Malice," General Vaine "Grinder," the **Sons of Animosity**, the **Daughters of Deprivation.** Two (**Null** and **Vaine**) betray him. Your arc villains / boss tier. *(Full nature & roster below.)*
3. **The Grey Makers** (the Lieutenants). The field officers who reach into the world — numerous, operational, your recurring mid-tier villains. **They are Silas's creations and answer to him specifically** — not to the Falseones. Their **core is the stigmatized Eversouls Silas corrupted** (turned against their own home); the ranks **grew with other fallen souls** since. This is why they "wear familiar faces and whisper with intimacy" — *the founding Grey Makers are family.*
   - **Silas** sits in his **own tier — beside the ladder, between Falseone and Lieutenant** — because he commands a **unique private unit no one else does:** the turned Eversouls. Outranks the Lieutenants (they're his); denied the Generals' tier (A'thule's distrust). See his entry below.
4. **Stigmants** (the infantry). Mindless products of the spread, pouring from rifts (see Section 7).

> **The intimate scale:** beneath the hierarchy sits **the Fallen Median** — someone the heroes may have known, lost to Stigma; the threat the heroes themselves could become. Not a rank in A'thule's army, but its most personal cost.

### Silas — The Betrayer Brother **[LOCKED]**
The Eversoul brother (Emerald Essence of **Illusion**) whose betrayal Stigmatized many of his siblings in the War for Ne'haris (see Section 2). To the Elloyds and Maisel, Silas is the **personal face of the cosmic threat** — a betrayer with a face they half-remember, a brother.

> **The author of the Grey Makers [LOCKED]:** Silas's true crime is bigger than personal betrayal — **he tainted his own Eversoul siblings with Stigma and turned them into the Grey Makers**, an army of corrupted kin aimed *inward against their own home.* He didn't just fall; he **manufactured A'thule's entire Lieutenant tier out of his own family**, and commands them as his personal unit. This is *why* the war devoured Ne'haris from within (see Section 2 / 9.5).

- **Rank [LOCKED]:** Silas sits in a **tier of his own — beside the ladder, between Falseone and Grey Maker.** He's no General (A'thule won't trust a kin-betrayer that far) but no mere Lieutenant either: he **commands a unique private unit no one else does** — the turned Eversouls (the Grey Makers). A'thule keeps him under-promoted because a being who betrayed his own *kin* will, by A'thule's logic, **betray anyone.** Bitter irony: Silas *delivered an entire army of corrupted Eversouls* and is **still** capped below the Falseones.
- **Motive [LOCKED]:** this galls Silas endlessly. He is forever **trying to prove his worth** — which makes him the antagonist most likely to overreach, freelance, and stage bold, unsanctioned moves to earn the rank he believes he's owed. He *auditions* rather than waits; he's the one who personally collides with the heroes.
- **The double exile [LOCKED theme]:** he betrayed his way *out* of one family (the Eversouls) and into a second (A'thule's forces) that trusts him even less. Twice an outsider — and the second time permanent, by his own crime.
- **His unit, the Grey Makers:** his corrupted siblings are *his* to command; he drives them recklessly to manufacture the wins he needs to prove himself. The heroes likely meet his **Grey Makers and schemes before they meet him** — and may face a Grey Maker who *was* family.
- **The crack in A'thule's army [HOOK]:** A'thule is probably *right* to distrust him — Silas's resentment is a fault line that could be turned. **Omin's Aether sight could read that resentment**; the betrayer who might betray the betrayer is a card held for later.
- **The intimacy of it:** facing A'thule's forces may mean facing their own brother, and Omin may **recognize him** when no one else can.
- **The cruelest version of Omin's arc:** she hunts the truth of Ne'haris; the truth is *"our brother betrayed us"*; and the proof stands before her wearing a face the world won't believe — *the gap between what she perceives and what she can prove, at maximum.*
- **The contrast [LOCKED]:** his Restoration siblings became **Neversouls** (fell but escaped full corruption); Silas is marked **corrupted** — the betrayer who *caused* the others' fall, the Mind-child of *Illusion* who is to the Eversouls what A'thule is to the Paramounts.
- **Why he persists [LOCKED]:** Silas **refused the Ley.** Where the cleansed are erased and the Neversouls merely escaped, Silas *rejected* return entirely — which is exactly why he endures as a **Corrupted Stigmatized Neversoul** in A'thule's employ. (Mirror of A'thule refusing his own cage; refusal is what lets corruption keep its self.)

## 9.5 The Present Age — The Inciting Event **[LOCKED]**

For generations, **Vi'talitas was an ordinary world** — mystical races coexisting, anima-rich but stable, its deeper secrets not much troubling daily life. Then its **fabric gave way.** A **massive rift** tore open, entangling Vi'talitas with what lay beyond its borders, and **Stigma poured through** — a corruptive, suffocating miasma whose mere presence burns the eyes and mouth, ranging in harm from minimal to lethal by exposure. Those who gave in became **Stigmants**, and they flooded the world as the rift widened, consuming or corrupting everything alive — people, beasts, plants, even the raw elements twisting into something perverse.

**At that darkest hour, the Medians awakened.** Dormant anima rose in people across every region — those with the natural affinity to draw on the Ley and shape it into offense, defense, and restoration, and to fight the Stigmants. As the Medians rose, **flashes of past-life memory flooded back** into the population, and **the Polestar — nucleus of the Ley — awakened**, and its people with it. Vi'talitas began returning to a state its people had only read about in ancient tomes.

**One group of Medians sealed the great breach**, saving the newly reborn world at the cost of their anonymity — remembered only as **Type Genesis**, the first to show how far Animancy could grow. Through their early research it was **confirmed that Vi'talitas was once Ne'haris.** Generations on, those events are now carved into legend.

Today, **Stigmants are a constant threat but no longer an apocalyptic one.** Rifts still open here and there, but now there are Medians to subdue them and Generics to support the cause. *This is the world the series opens in.*

> **Type Genesis [LOCKED] [HOOK]:** the nameless first Medians who sealed the original breach and proved Animancy's potential — and confirmed Vi'talitas = Ne'haris. A ready-made legend, a possible secret lineage, and a title later Medians' "Types" may echo.
> **Connects to:** the Median awakening here is the in-world reason the whole Median/Generic society (Section 7) exists; the returning past-life memories are the memory-casting mechanic (Section 3) switching on at civilizational scale.
